# Compliance research brief — vetting, training, RTW, insurance, contracts

- **Researched:** 2026-07-07 (sources are 2024-2026 official guidance; every claim cited)
- **Purpose:** grounds Roadmap v2 (Docs/roadmap-v2.md). Items marked UNCERTAIN need a solicitor.

## 1. Mandatory training — domiciliary care workers

- There is no single statutory list. "Mandatory" = what CQC/Skills for Care expect evidenced. Skills for Care publishes the **Statutory and Mandatory Training Guide** (latest Dec 2025) [skillsforcare.org.uk/...Statutory-and-mandatory-training.aspx].
- **Care Certificate (15 standards)** is the induction baseline: role, personal development, duty of care, equality & diversity, person-centred care, communication, privacy & dignity, fluids & nutrition, mental health/dementia/LD awareness, safeguarding adults, safeguarding children, basic life support, health & safety, handling information, infection prevention [Care-Certificate-Standards.pdf].
- Sector-standard mandatory list: health & safety, safeguarding adults (+children), fire safety, equality & diversity, infection prevention & control, moving & handling of people, food hygiene & nutrition, basic life support/first aid, medication, MCA/DoLS, information governance [redcrier.com mandatory-training-framework; theaccessgroup.com].
- **Refresher periods: annual-for-everything is NOT law.** Norms: basic life support at least annually; first aid every 3 years; most others at least every 3 years unless risk changes. HSWA 1974 + MHOR 1992 + MHSWR 1999 require training "adequate and up to date" per risk assessment; annual moving-&-handling refreshers are widespread best practice, not statute [hse.gov.uk/healthservices/moving-handling].
- Medication competency annual review is common practice per NICE NG67 expectations — best practice, UNCERTAIN as hard requirement for non-registered models.

## 2. Nurses in home care

- Same H&S training expectations as carers, plus clinical updates; agencies typically require annual mandatory-training-day equivalents.
- **NMC revalidation: every 3 years** — 450 practice hours, 35h CPD (20 participatory), 5 feedback pieces, 5 reflective accounts, reflective discussion, confirmer sign-off, health/character + indemnity declarations. Evidence is self-held; submitted only if selected for verification [nmc.org.uk how-to-revalidate-booklet.pdf].
- **Statement of entry** confirms qualifications and initial registration date — it is NOT proof of current registration. Verify live status via the NMC online register (PIN check) [nmc.org.uk/registration/your-registration/statement-of-entry].
- **Indemnity:** self-employed nurses must hold their own professional indemnity arrangement (employer cover doesn't extend to private work); it's an NMC registration condition [revalidation.nmc.org.uk professional-indemnity-arrangement].

## 3. Clinical skills checklist (community/agency nurses)

Standard self-rated (novice/competent/expert) categories [Harley Street Nurses checklist; medbridge.com]:
- Medication: oral/topical/PR/PV, IM/SC injections, IV therapy & pumps, controlled drugs, syringe drivers
- Enteral feeding: PEG/NG care & feeding, enteral meds
- Airway: tracheostomy care & suctioning, oxygen, nebulisers
- Continence: urinary catheterisation (M/F/suprapubic) & catheter care, bowel/stoma care
- Wounds: dressings, pressure ulcer prevention/grading, aseptic technique
- Long-term conditions: diabetes (BG monitoring, insulin), epilepsy incl. rescue meds, end-of-life/palliative
- Assessment: vital signs/NEWS2, moving & handling with equipment, documentation, venepuncture

## 4. Right to work (2025-26 rules)

- **British/Irish passport:** manual check of the passport (current OR expired) is sufficient alone; keep a dated copy. No other documents needed [gov.uk employers-guide-to-right-to-work-checks, 26 Jun 2025 edition].
- **Everyone else:** the Home Office **online share-code check is the sole and sufficient method** (share code + DOB at gov.uk/view-right-to-work). **No visa or BRP copy is needed** — retain the online check result page and verify the photo. BRPs stopped being issued Oct 2024 (now eVisas); expired BRPs unacceptable since Jan 2026; share codes valid 90 days.
- **Self-employed nuance:** civil penalties currently apply to employees only — BUT the Border Security, Asylum and Immigration Act 2025 extends RTW duties to gig/self-employed workers, expected in force ~Oct 2026. Platforms already check everyone in practice. Build it as mandatory now.

## 5. Insurance & liability

- Requiring self-employed carers to hold their own public liability + professional indemnity is the sector norm for keeping liability off the platform. Two models:
  - **Carer-holds-own:** standalone policies are cheap and standard (£5m public liability + PI from ~£89/year, e.g. Surewise/Fish).
  - **Platform-arranged group policy (Curam):** £1m PL + £1m professional negligence auto-activated per platform-billed visit — doubles as an anti-disintermediation carrot (cover only applies to on-platform work).
- PrimeCarers insures carers via its provider, funded from commission. TrustonTap: carers responsible for own arrangements (detail unpublished, UNCERTAIN).
- Nurses: own indemnity is already an NMC condition — platform's job is to collect evidence of it (we already have the `insurance` document type).

## 6. Contracts & non-solicitation

- **Standard structure (Curam model):** platform T&Cs with carer + platform T&Cs with client + a direct client↔carer Service Agreement from a platform-provided template (platform is not a party) [curamcare.com/tscs].
- **Anti-circumvention:** Curam charges £1,000+VAT for off-platform engagement of an introduced carer; £750+VAT "buy-out" allowed after 90 days of on-platform billing. Reasonably-scoped fees (time-limited, genuine pre-estimate of lost commission) are the accepted form; enforceability is penalty-clause dependent, no care-sector case law found (UNCERTAIN).
- **Red flags (status & CQC):**
  - CQC exemption survives only with **no ongoing direction/control** of care after introduction [cqc.org.uk personal-care-ongoing-role-introductory-agencies].
  - HMRC ESM4015: many "self-employed" live-in carers fail status tests; platform-set rates, mandated payment terms, rostering, supervising or disciplining all point to control (employment-status risk for clients, CQC risk for us). Payment *facilitation* is safer than payment *mandates* — line untested (UNCERTAIN).
  - **Introduction agencies likely fall under the Employment Agencies Act 1973 / Conduct Regulations 2003**, whose fee restrictions on charging work-seekers are directly relevant to a 15% carer-side commission (how Curam/PrimeCarers structure this lawfully is not public). **Priority solicitor question — touches DR-0001.**
