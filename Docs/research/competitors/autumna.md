# Autumna (autumna.co.uk) — Competitive Intelligence Report

**One-liner:** Bootstrapped, founder-led "Rightmove of social care" — a free-to-families UK later-life care directory + human advice line covering care homes, home care, live-in care and retirement living, monetised via **flat-rate monthly premium-profile subscriptions** from providers (£33–£75/month +VAT) with an explicit, marketing-led "no referral fees, ever" stance. ~£1.4m turnover (2023), 22 staff, no disclosed external funding.

**Scope note for CareMatch:** Like Lottie, Autumna is NOT a carer marketplace — it lists **regulated providers** (CQC-registered agencies, homes, retirement operators), never self-employed individual carers. Its value here is as an acquisition-and-monetisation case study: the cheapest-possible provider-side pricing model in the space, plus a very effective human-advice + data-content demand engine.

**Research caveat:** autumna.co.uk itself is behind aggressive Cloudflare bot protection (WebFetch and curl both blocked), so on-site copy below is reconstructed from search-index snippets of Autumna pages and third-party sources rather than full page fetches. Facts are cited; anything inferred is flagged.

## 1. Business model

- **Who pays whom:** Families pay **nothing** — "The website is free to use, we are proudly independent, and we never take referral fees" ([autumna.co.uk homepage snippet](https://www.autumna.co.uk/), [West Berkshire council directory listing](https://directory.westberks.gov.uk/directory-search/details/?id=ba9ccfee-8272-4ce1-b79f-b195011288f9)). Care providers pay a flat monthly subscription for an enhanced ("Premium") profile; leads are unlimited and included ([Care England smart directory entry](https://smartdirectory.careengland.org.uk/supporter-member/22/autumna)).
- **Directory, not marketplace.** Autumna lists *all* CQC-registered providers by default (basic listing seeded from regulator data — inferred from "an impartial service that lists ALL providers", [autumna.co.uk](https://www.autumna.co.uk/)), then upsells providers to claim and upgrade their profile ([how to claim and edit your profile](https://www.autumna.co.uk/how-claim-and-edit-your-profile/)). No transaction, no booking, no take-rate — introductions end at the enquiry.
- **Revenue streams:**
  1. **Premium Profile subscriptions** (core) — flat monthly fee per branch/home, "all pre-qualified leads are included within a fixed monthly fee", no per-lead or per-placement charges ([Care England directory](https://smartdirectory.careengland.org.uk/supporter-member/22/autumna)).
  2. **OpenScore widget** — a real-time quality score (out of 10) providers can embed on their own site: **£99 +VAT first year** standalone; included in premium listings ([Care England directory](https://smartdirectory.careengland.org.uk/supporter-member/22/autumna), [openscore.org.uk/autumna-for-care-homes](https://www.openscore.org.uk/autumna-for-care-homes)).
  3. **B2B employee benefit** — an eldercare advice benefit sold via platforms like Vivup and The Care Workers' Charity ([autumna.co.uk/elder-care-employee-benefit-scheme/](https://www.autumna.co.uk/elder-care-employee-benefit-scheme/)). Pricing not public.
  4. Heritage consultancy (Chosen with Care, bespoke search for self-funders since 2009) folded into the brand ([about-us](https://www.autumna.co.uk/about/about-us/)).
- **Anti-referral-fee positioning is the brand.** Debbie Harris: referral fees "would lead to bias towards some care homes and pointing people to the wrong homes"; "the only thing that gets you to the top of the list is the amount of information you have" ([Care Home Professional interview](https://www.carehomeprofessional.com/interview-autumna-the-story-of-the-uks-social-care-comparison-site/)). This is a deliberate contrast with pay-per-lead rivals (Lottie, carehome.co.uk).
- **Ranking = data flywheel.** Search-result ordering is governed by "Profile Strength" — the completeness of the provider's information, explicitly including pricing ([carehomeprofessional interview](https://www.carehomeprofessional.com/interview-autumna-the-story-of-the-uks-social-care-comparison-site/); [ElderIndex glossary](https://www.elderindex.co.uk/glossary)). Aligned incentive: providers volunteer rich data (prices, facilities) to rank higher, which makes the directory more useful and gives Autumna a proprietary market-price dataset.

## 2. Pricing

**Families: £0** — free directory, free advice line, free 24-hour shortlisting service ([free-advice-lines/care-advice](https://www.autumna.co.uk/free-advice-lines/care-advice/), [West Berkshire directory](https://directory.westberks.gov.uk/directory-search/details/?id=ba9ccfee-8272-4ce1-b79f-b195011288f9)).

**Providers — Premium Profile, flat monthly, +VAT** (per Care England smart directory, the most itemised public source):
- **Home care agency: £33/month +VAT** ([Care England smart directory](https://smartdirectory.careengland.org.uk/supporter-member/22/autumna))
- **Live-in care branch: £58/month +VAT** (same source)
- **Care home: £58/month +VAT** per the Care England entry, but **£75/month +VAT** per OpenScore's page ("The cost is £75+VAT per month for a Premium Listing on Autumna" — [openscore.org.uk/autumna-for-care-homes](https://www.openscore.org.uk/autumna-for-care-homes)). Discrepancy is likely a price rise over time (inferred — the £58 source is older); treat current care-home price as ~£75/month.
- **Terms:** "flat rate for every premium profile", no group discounts, no referral fees; premium includes "unlimited leads direct to your inbox", direct phone routing, website traffic, OpenScore, Live Ratings ([Care England](https://smartdirectory.careengland.org.uk/supporter-member/22/autumna), [OpenScore](https://www.openscore.org.uk/autumna-for-care-homes)).
- **OpenScore widget standalone: £99 +VAT/first year** ([Care England](https://smartdirectory.careengland.org.uk/supporter-member/22/autumna)).

**Benchmark implication:** at £33–£75/month, Autumna is an order of magnitude cheaper for providers than pay-per-lead models (Lottie-style qualified enquiries in this sector are typically tens of pounds *each* — inferred comparison). Autumna is competing on price + trust, not lead volume guarantees.

## 3. Client acquisition (families)

- **SEO / content is the primary engine** (inferred from footprint; no public traffic figures found — Similarweb/Ahrefs data not retrievable this session):
  - **Cost-data content built on their own dataset:** "How much are care home costs per week in 2025?" (avg ~£1,100/week, regional breakdowns), "Care home costs Wales", "How to Avoid Care Home Costs" ([blog](https://www.autumna.co.uk/blog/how-much-are-care-home-costs-per-week-in-2025/)); home care avg "£22–£23/hour" published from their listings data ([find-home-care](https://www.autumna.co.uk/find-home-care/)). Because Profile Strength rewards providers for publishing prices, Autumna owns price data competitors don't have — content moat feeding SEO and PR.
  - **Advice Centre** evergreen guides: "Home Care explained", "Care Home services explained", funding-care guides ([advice-centre](https://www.autumna.co.uk/advice-centre/home-care-explained/), [funding-care](https://www.autumna.co.uk/care-advice/funding-care/)).
  - **Programmatic directory pages** by location, care type, brand and provider ([/directory/](https://www.autumna.co.uk/directory/), [/directory/live-in-care/by-provider/](https://www.autumna.co.uk/directory/live-in-care/by-provider/)).
- **Paid search:** they retain an agency for dedicated SEO **and PPC** — "a new PPC strategy was required to ensure Autumna was appearing front and centre of people's searches" ([the human tech agency case study](https://www.thehumantech.agency/work/autumna)). Spend/results not public.
- **Human advice line as converter and differentiator:** free, "impartial", **01892 335 330, open 7 days a week** (Mon–Fri 8.30–17.30, Sat 10–17, Sun 10–16); advisors run a database search and deliver a **shortlist within 24 hours** ([free-advice-lines](https://www.autumna.co.uk/free-advice-lines/care-advice/), [West Berkshire](https://directory.westberks.gov.uk/directory-search/details/?id=ba9ccfee-8272-4ce1-b79f-b195011288f9)). They call themselves "the UK's only website and advice line specialising in self-funding families looking for elder care" ([OpenScore page](https://www.openscore.org.uk/autumna-for-care-homes)).
- **Council & community directory placement (free distribution):** Autumna is listed as a signposting resource in local-authority directories — West Berkshire Directory, Kent Connect to Support, YourCircle ([westberks.gov.uk](https://directory.westberks.gov.uk/directory-search/details/?id=ba9ccfee-8272-4ce1-b79f-b195011288f9), [kent.connecttosupport.org](https://kent.connecttosupport.org/search/community-directory/?id=db70bb03-ec14-4d64-bbb7-b0bd00f94afa), [yourcircle.org.uk](https://www.yourcircle.org.uk/Services/15157)).
- **Professional referral network:** a formal "Referring Clients to Autumna" programme aimed at "care professionals, GPs, hospital discharge teams, solicitors, accountants, IFAs" — referral by simply emailing the client's details ([referring-clients-to-autumna](https://www.autumna.co.uk/referring-clients-to-autumna/)). Providers themselves are encouraged to refer families they can't serve.
- **Employer channel:** eldercare employee-benefit scheme distributed via Vivup and The Care Workers' Charity ([elder-care-employee-benefit-scheme-vivup](https://www.autumna.co.uk/elder-care-employee-benefit-scheme-vivup/), [thecareworkerscharity.org.uk](https://www.thecareworkerscharity.org.uk/employee-benefit-scheme/)).
- **PR via data + sector controversy:** e.g. analysis that CQC ratings are "irrelevant to families" because so many are stale ([The Carer coverage](https://thecareruk.com/cqc-ratings-irrelevant-to-families-analysis-reveals/)); nationwide-first interactive map of real-time care-home ratings (Nov 2025) covered by trade press ([carehomeprofessional.com](https://www.carehomeprofessional.com/interactive-care-home-ratings-map/), [National Care Forum](https://www.nationalcareforum.org.uk/members-news/interactive-map-featuring-real-time-care-home-ratings-launched-in-nationwide-first/)). Founder is a visible sector voice (LaingBuisson events speaker, Silver Marketing Association, awards sponsorship) ([laingbuissonevents.com](https://www.laingbuissonevents.com/speaker/debbie-harris/)).

## 4. Supply side (providers)

- **Cold-start solved by listing everyone:** all CQC-registered services appear (12,376 home care providers; 26,000+ total services across care homes, retirement, home care, live-in — [find-home-care](https://www.autumna.co.uk/find-home-care/), [about-us](https://www.autumna.co.uk/about/about-us/); one source says "over 50,000 providers" including retirement living — [CareTech Index](https://bridgeheadcommunications.com/caretech-index/autumna); figures vary by what's counted).
- **Provider pitch:** "Over 90% of self-funding care seekers are researching care online" → premium profile = "a reliable source of marketing with greater levels of enquiries from self-funded care seekers, resulting in increased bed occupancy or service users" ([Care England smart directory](https://smartdirectory.careengland.org.uk/supporter-member/22/autumna), [for-care-providers](https://www.autumna.co.uk/about/autumna-for-care-providers/)). Key hooks: self-funder-only audience (highest-margin clients), flat predictable fee, no referral fees, no group discounts (SMEs pay the same as chains — fairness signal).
- **Provider tooling:** self-serve profile claim/edit; dashboard stats and monthly email performance summaries ([for-care-providers page snippet](https://www.autumna.co.uk/about/autumna-for-care-providers/)); Live Ratings — post-visit feedback (8 questions) publishing instantly to premium profiles via integrations with e-Reception Book and Person Centred Software ([autumna blog](https://www.autumna.co.uk/blog/care-home-reviews-e-reception-book-and-autumna/), [LaingBuisson News](https://www.laingbuissonnews.com/care-markets-content/news/person-centred-software-and-autumna-partner-to-create-live-ratings/)); OpenScore quality score; S.A.F.E. (COVID-era infection-control data badge, launched 2020 — [TechRound interview](https://techround.co.uk/interviews/autumna-debbie/)).
- **Trade-body embedding as a sales channel:** supporter member of Care England, partner of National Care Forum, PRSB partner ([careengland.org.uk/autumna](https://www.careengland.org.uk/autumna/), [nationalcareforum.org.uk/partners/autumna](https://www.nationalcareforum.org.uk/partners/autumna/), [theprsb.org](https://theprsb.org/news/care-provider-directory-autumna-becomes-a-prsb-partner/)).

## 5. Website / product teardown

- **Positioning:** "The UK's largest & most detailed later-life living and elderly care directory" ([autumna.co.uk](https://www.autumna.co.uk/)); consumer promise = impartiality + detail + free human help. Explicitly self-funder-focused.
- **Funnel:** search by care type (care homes / home care / live-in / retirement living) and location → filterable results (services, facilities, specialisms like dementia/respite) ordered by Profile Strength → provider profile with pricing, CQC rating, OpenScore, Live Ratings → enquire direct (premium providers get direct phone routing). Parallel human path: call the advice line or request a shortlist, delivered within 24h. ([find-care-homes](https://www.autumna.co.uk/find-care-homes/), [live-ratings](https://www.autumna.co.uk/live-ratings/), [free-advice-lines](https://www.autumna.co.uk/free-advice-lines/care-advice/))
- **Trust signals:** "proudly independent, never take referral fees" repeated everywhere; CQC ratings shown alongside OpenScore and S.A.F.E.; Trustpilot badge — currently "Excellent" 4.7/5 (~100–130 reviews; an earlier snapshot showed 4.0 with a Trustpilot notice that the company "may be asking for reviews in a way Trustpilot doesn't support" — point-in-time flag, currently rated Excellent) ([trustpilot.com/review/autumna.co.uk](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/autumna.co.uk)).
- **Notable features:** Profile Strength ranking; published market-price averages per area; interactive real-time ratings map (2025, sector first — [carehomeprofessional.com](https://www.carehomeprofessional.com/interactive-care-home-ratings-map/)); OpenScore embeddable widget (gets Autumna a branded backlink on provider websites — smart SEO loop, inferred benefit).
- **Weak spots observed:** heavy Cloudflare gating (blocks archiving/scrapers, possibly some legitimate agents/AI answer engines — inferred risk for AEO); directory-era UI conventions rather than a guided marketplace flow; the small review count (~130) is thin for a business claiming national scale.

## 6. Regulatory stance

- Autumna is a **signposting/introduction directory, not a care provider** — it holds no CQC registration for delivering care and takes no role in the care contract. (Its Companies House SIC code is nonetheless 87300 "Residential care activities for the elderly and disabled" — a mis-set code rather than a registration; inferred, [Companies House](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/10879272).)
- It **leans on CQC data** (lists all CQC-registered providers, displays inspection ratings) while **publicly critiquing CQC ratings as stale**, positioning its Live Ratings/OpenScore as the "real-time alternative to CQC ratings" ([autumna blog](https://www.autumna.co.uk/blog/new-partnership-delivers-real-time-alternative-to-cqc-ratings/), [The Carer](https://thecareruk.com/cqc-ratings-irrelevant-to-families-analysis-reveals/)). Lesson: a directory can be simultaneously CQC-anchored (credibility) and CQC-supplementing (differentiation).

## 8. Strengths, weaknesses, lessons for CareMatch

**Strengths**
- Trust positioning ("impartial, no referral fees, flat fee") that is genuinely differentiated in a sector rife with referral-fee scepticism — and it doubles as PR.
- Profile-Strength ranking: a single mechanic that solves ranking fairness, provider data collection, and price-data moat simultaneously.
- Hybrid human+digital: the 7-day advice line and 24h shortlist convert distressed, time-poor families that pure directories lose.
- Free full-market seeding from CQC data = instant comprehensiveness, SEO surface area, and an upsell base.
- Capital-efficient: ~£1.4m revenue, 22 staff, no VC — profitable-shaped growth (inferred).

**Weaknesses**
- Low ARPU flat-fee model caps revenue; no per-placement upside, no transaction layer. Lottie's pay-per-enquiry monetises the same demand far more aggressively.
- Directory revenue depends on Google; PPC dependence admitted ([human tech agency](https://www.thehumantech.agency/work/autumna)). Aggressive bot-blocking may hurt AI-search visibility.
- Phone-heavy service model scales linearly with headcount.
- Thin public review base (~130 Trustpilot reviews) and a past review-solicitation flag.
- No coverage of self-employed carers — the fastest-growing, cheapest segment of home care is invisible to it (opportunity for CareMatch).

**Lessons for CareMatch**
1. **Publish your price data.** Autumna's incentive design (rank higher if you publish prices) created a proprietary cost dataset that powers its best-performing SEO/PR content ("care home costs per week"). CareMatch should surface real carer hourly rates per area from day one and publish periodic cost reports.
2. **A human shortlist with a 24-hour SLA is a cheap, powerful converter.** Even a solo founder-run "advice line" (callback + shortlist email) would differentiate a self-serve marketplace at CareMatch's stage and qualify demand.
3. **Free distribution via councils and discharge-adjacent professionals works.** Get listed in local-authority community directories (Connect to Support, county directories) and build a one-email referral path for social prescribers, discharge teams, and solicitors/IFAs — Autumna proves these channels accept an introduction service.
4. **"No referral fees / impartial" is a monetisation trade-off, not just ethics.** It buys trust and provider goodwill but caps ARPU at ~£33–£75/month. CareMatch's introduction-fee model can out-monetise this — but expect Autumna-style "we're the impartial one" attacks; design fee transparency to neutralise them.
5. **Benchmark: home-care providers' willingness to pay a directory is ~£33/month for unlimited leads.** Any CareMatch fee perceived as "listing/marketing" (rather than tied to a successful match) will be judged against that anchor — another argument for success-based pricing on the family or match side.
