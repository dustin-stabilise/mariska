# Care Sourcer (caresourcer.com) — Competitive Intelligence Report

**TL;DR:** Edinburgh startup (founded 2015/16) that built a free "compare-the-market for elderly care" — families enter a postcode, CQC/Care Inspectorate-registered providers respond with availability. Raised ~£9m+ including a record £8.5m Scottish Series A (Legal & General £6m + ADV £2.5m, 2018). Never owned the care transaction: revenue depended on provider-side lead/partnership fees and NHS discharge contracts. Pivoted through directory acquisitions (Good Care Guide, Care Home Advisor, 2019), a Covid taskforce, and B2B partnerships (St James's Place), then sold its brand/assets to KareHero — a 2022-founded employer-paid care-concierge startup — in **July 2023**. The original company was renamed AWPAPM Ltd in April 2023 and **dissolved 8 July 2025**. Today caresourcer.com survives as an SEO directory + lead funnel feeding KareHero's concierge business.

---

## 1. Business model

- **Model: comparison/matching marketplace between families and regulated care *businesses*** (care homes + home-care agencies) — NOT a carer-level introduction agency like HomeTouch/Curam. Care Sourcer matched demand to CQC/Care Inspectorate-registered providers; the actual care contract, employment, and payment all happened off-platform.
- **Free for families, monetised on the supply/B2B side.** "Care Sourcer provides an entirely free care matching service, searching all 27,000 registered providers to bring the best possible options within 24 hours" (marketing copy quoted in search results; also [Dealroom](https://app.dealroom.co/companies/care_sourcer): "The platform is free to use for consumers, generating revenue through partnerships and service agreements with care providers").
- **Revenue streams (assembled from public sources; exact mix never disclosed):**
  1. **Provider-side fees** — "partnerships and service agreements with care providers" ([Dealroom](https://app.dealroom.co/companies/care_sourcer)). Today the provider page offers a **free profile** with direct enquiries ([caresourcer.com/providers](https://www.caresourcer.com/providers/)); whether/how much providers paid for leads or premium placement historically is **not public** — inferred to be lead-gen/premium-listing style, never confirmed as a take rate.
  2. **NHS contracts** — a **£757k NHS trust deal** in 2017 ([Insider.co.uk](https://www.insider.co.uk/news/tech-start-up-care-sourcer-11491589)) and an NHS England contract ([The Scotsman, 2017](http://www.scotsman.com/business/companies/tech/health-tech-start-up-lands-contract-with-nhs-england-1-4348352)). Product "Care Sourcer Pro" embedded a Care Sourcer staffer in hospitals to source care for discharge patients; development commissioned by NHS England / SBRI. Claimed result: **40% reduction in delayed transfers of care** (Care Sourcer/KareHero marketing; also "reducing delayed discharge rates in pilot NHS hospitals by 40%" — [caresourcer.com/aboutus](https://www.caresourcer.com/aboutus/)).
  3. **Financial-services partnerships** — strategic investor Legal & General (whose Retirement Retail arm wanted later-life care journeys attached to its annuity/equity-release customers), and a **May 2021 partnership with St James's Place** to support wealth clients' long-term care needs (search result citing 2021 coverage). Note: L&G later built its own in-house telephone "[Care Concierge](https://www.legalandgeneral.com/retirement/care/care-concierge/)" service — a sign the strategic partner didn't ultimately route its volume through Care Sourcer (**inferred**).
- **No commission on care delivered, no payments on-platform.** This is the core structural weakness (see §8).

## 2. Pricing

- **Families: £0.** Every source is consistent that the consumer service was and is entirely free ([caresourcer.com](https://www.caresourcer.com/), Dealroom). No subscription, no booking fee, no visible paid tier ever.
- **Instead of pricing, the funnel captures leads:** postcode search → compare providers ("compare care providers in your area on price, ratings and specialisms") → contact providers directly or submit a request; plus a "[Care support / Care Concierge](https://www.caresourcer.com/care-concierge/)" form promising "expert resources personalised to your needs, straight to your email" from "in-house UK care experts" with "over 40 years of experience... between them". No prices anywhere on the consumer site.
- **Carers/providers:** providers get a **"free profile"** — "Complete our short online form to claim your free profile... Connect with people looking for care in your area" ([caresourcer.com/providers](https://www.caresourcer.com/providers/)). No published lead cost, commission %, or subscription tier; contact is providers@caresourcer.com. Historic paid-lead pricing (if any) was never published — **unknown**.
- **NHS pricing:** contract-based (the £757k trust deal above); no rate card public.

## 3. Client acquisition (families)

- **SEO is the dominant visible channel.** Massive programmatic footprint: location pages for "care homes in {town}", "home care in {town}", "dementia care in {town}" across 16+ UK cities in the homepage footer, e.g. [/s/providers/towns/edinburgh/home-care](https://www.caresourcer.com/s/providers/towns/edinburgh/home-care), [/homecare/london/](https://www.caresourcer.com/homecare/london/), plus a large "[care support](https://www.caresourcer.com/caresupport/understanding-care/self-discharge-from-hospital/)" advice library targeting informational queries (hospital discharge, CHC funding, power of attorney, refusing discharge). At acquisition, KareHero called it "the **2nd most visited consumer website in the UK focused on elderly care**" ([KareHero LinkedIn, Jul 2023](https://www.linkedin.com/company/care-sourcer-limited)). Similarweb groups it with carehome.co.uk (~626k visits/mo, Nov 2024), trustedcare.co.uk and carechoices.co.uk ([Similarweb](https://www.similarweb.com/website/caresourcer.com/competitors/)) — caresourcer.com's own current traffic is materially smaller than carehome.co.uk's (**inferred from its position in that comparison set**).
- **Directory acquisitions to buy traffic/content:** October 2019, acquired **Good Care Guide** and **Care Home Advisor** from LaingBuisson — "This acquisition demonstrates our commitment to becoming the UK's marketplace for quality care for the elderly" (Andrew Parfery, [Digit.fyi](https://www.digit.fyi/care-sourcer-double-acquisition-social-care-websites/)); press dubbed it the "'Rightmove of care'" ([Insider.co.uk](https://www.insider.co.uk/news/rightmove-care-snaps-up-two-20582592)).
- **NHS / hospital-discharge channel:** Care Sourcer Pro placed staff inside discharge units — acquisition at the point of maximum urgency. Also a 2018 tie-up with Cultivate to help the NHS provide elderly care ([Insider.co.uk](https://www.insider.co.uk/news/nhs-elderly-care-sources-cultivate-13386833)).
- **PR:** heavy Scottish tech press (record Series A), Covid-19 "taskforce" launch in March 2020 ([The Scotsman](https://www.scotsman.com/health/coronavirus/coronavirus-edinburgh-based-care-sourcer-launches-covid-19-taskforce-2522928)), free wellbeing support for "800,000 UK care workers" with L&G in Sept 2019 ([L&G press release](https://group.legalandgeneral.com/newsroom/press-releases/2019/9/care-sourcer-launches-free-health-and-wellbeing-support-for-over-800-000-uk-care-workers/)).
- **Growth hires:** ex-Skyscanner marketing director Ilana Munckton joined the management team (~2018) ([Insider.co.uk](https://www.insider.co.uk/news/former-skyscanner-director-ilana-munckton-14023084)) — signals a consumer-growth/SEO playbook borrowed from travel comparison.
- **Paid ads:** no strong public evidence either way — **unknown**.

## 4. Carer supply side

- **Care Sourcer never recruited individual carers** — its supply was **registered care businesses** (~"all 27,000 registered providers" claimed). So there was no carer vetting, carer earnings, or carer take-home to manage; vetting was outsourced to the regulator (CQC / Care Inspectorate / Disclosure Scotland badges shown on-site).
- **Provider pitch:** free profile, "thousands of people in need use Care Sourcer each month," direct enquiries, performance reporting ([/providers](https://www.caresourcer.com/providers/)). Onboarding friction very low (short online form) — consistent with a directory that needs listing breadth more than provider revenue (**observation**).
- **Implication for CareMatch:** Care Sourcer's model is the *agency-matching* cousin of ours, not a carer marketplace — but its monetisation failure on the supply side is directly relevant (§8).

## 5. Website/product teardown (current site, under KareHero)

- **Headline:** "Find elderly care in your area" / "Our qualified care experts are on hand to help" ([caresourcer.com](https://www.caresourcer.com/)).
- **Funnel:** (1) "Enter your postcode" → (2) "Review your options" (compare on price, ratings, specialisms) → (3) "Choose the right provider" (contact directly or batch-request). Parallel funnel: the Care Concierge form → email nurture → human care-expert callback → (now) feeds KareHero's concierge/placement operation (**inferred**).
- **Trust signals:** CQC, Care Inspectorate, DBS and Disclosure Scotland logos; testimonials incl. NHS/provider voices; Reviews.co.uk widget — **4.8/5 from 251 reviews on Reviews.io** ([reviews.io](https://www.reviews.io/company-reviews/store/caresourcer-com1)) but only **2 reviews on Trustpilot** ([trustpilot.com](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/caresourcer.com)) — review volume is thin for a site claiming top-2 UK traffic (**observation**).
- **Product depth is shallow:** no accounts, no messaging, no payments, no care plans, no matching algorithm visible today — it's a directory + content library + lead forms. The clever product (Care Sourcer Pro, in-hospital sourcing with 48-hour matching) was B2B and is no longer promoted on the consumer site; the /nhs/ path now serves consumer content about NHS Continuing Healthcare ([caresourcer.com/nhs](https://www.caresourcer.com/nhs/)).
- **Footer:** "a brand owned by KareHero Ltd (company number 14314900), 128 City Road, London."

## 6. Regulatory stance

- **Neither a CQC-registered provider nor an introduction agency for individual carers.** Care Sourcer sat a layer above regulation: it introduced families to *already-regulated* providers and leaned on regulators' badges (CQC, Care Inspectorate, DBS, Disclosure Scotland) as its trust layer ([caresourcer.com](https://www.caresourcer.com/)). No registration of its own required; no care liability carried. Clean, cheap position — but it also meant no defensible role in the care relationship (**analysis**).

## 8. Why it failed / was absorbed — and lessons for CareMatch

**Why (synthesis, marked as analysis):**
1. **No ownership of the transaction.** Matching families to third-party agencies means zero take rate; the moment the intro happens, Care Sourcer is out of the loop. Monetisation collapsed to lead fees and partnerships — low-margin, churny, and capped.
2. **Supply side had no reason to pay.** UK care is demand-saturated and supply-constrained (workforce crisis). Agencies with full rosters don't buy leads; comparison pressure on price actively hurts them. A "Rightmove of care" needs providers hungry for demand — the market gave them the opposite.
3. **Free-consumer + B2B-partnership revenue is slow and lumpy.** NHS deals took years and pilots; the flagship strategic partner (L&G) ended up building its own Care Concierge in-house.
4. **Traffic ≠ business.** They genuinely won distribution (2nd-most-visited elderly-care site, directory roll-ups, SEO machine) and still couldn't convert it into a durable revenue model. The asset that survived — and what KareHero actually bought — was the traffic funnel, not the marketplace.

**Lessons for CareMatch:**
1. **Own the transaction.** Our introduction model must capture value at the match (family subscription/intro fee and/or carer-side fee tied to placements), with payments or at least booking commitment on-platform. Care Sourcer proves free matching with off-platform transactions doesn't fund a venture-scale business.
2. **Charge the side that feels scarcity.** In today's market, *families* feel scarcity (finding a good carer is hard); self-employed carers also value a steady client pipeline more than agencies did. Both are more chargeable than Care Sourcer's agency customers ever were — but validate willingness-to-pay before assuming carer-side fees.
3. **Copy the SEO playbook early — it works.** Programmatic town/service pages + an advice library around high-intent moments (hospital discharge, CHC funding, POA) got Care Sourcer to top-2 traffic. That content moat outlived the company. Build it from day one; it's the cheapest durable acquisition channel in this category.
4. **Hospital discharge is a real wedge but a brutal sales motion.** The 40% delayed-discharge result shows the value; the £757k-contract-then-quiet trajectory shows NHS procurement won't carry an early-stage P&L. Treat NHS/council channels as year-2+ upside, not core GTM.
5. **Have a B2B2C fallback in mind.** The version of this business that survives today is KareHero: employer-paid subscriptions for the same concierge/matching service. If direct consumer monetisation stalls, employers (and insurers/wealth managers — L&G, SJP kept circling this space) are the proven payers for care navigation.
