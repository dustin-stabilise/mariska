# Curam — Competitive Intelligence Report

**One-liner:** The UK's largest self-serve care introduction marketplace, matching families directly with self-employed, vetted carers, monetised via a dual-sided commission (~15% incl. VAT from carers + 12% from clients) — CareMatch's closest analogue and the benchmark for the category.

## 1. Business model

- **Core model:** Two-sided introduction marketplace. Families (or councils/NHS/case managers) hire self-employed carers directly; Curam provides matching, vetting, messaging/video, contracts, invoicing, escrow-style payments, and insurance. Carers are not Curam employees ([curamcare.com/how-it-works](https://www.curamcare.com/how-it-works), [carer FAQ](https://www.curamcare.com/carer-information)).
- **Revenue streams (dual-sided take):**
  - **Carer commission:** "Curam charges a fee of 15% on all shift invoices created on the platform" ([carer-information](https://www.curamcare.com/carer-information)). Curam's own blog frames this as "12.5% + VAT" — i.e. 15% including VAT, consistent ([blog: everything-you-need-to-know-about-curam](https://www.curamcare.com/blogs/everything-you-need-to-know-about-curam)).
  - **Client fee:** "12% client fee will be added to your invoices" ([client-information](https://www.curamcare.com/client-information)).
  - **Effective blended take rate:** competitor PrimeCarers calculates the total platform take at ~24.1% of client spend (client pays rate ×1.12, carer keeps rate ×0.85) ([primecarers.co.uk/live-in-care/providers](https://primecarers.co.uk/live-in-care/providers)). Arithmetic checks out: (1.12 − 0.85)/1.12 ≈ 24%.
  - **Off-platform circumvention penalty:** cash/off-platform payment is prohibited; breach triggers a **£1,000 + VAT placement fee** ([client-information](https://www.curamcare.com/client-information)). This is their disintermediation defence.
  - **B2B channel:** local authorities, NHS discharge teams, care agencies and case managers post jobs "at zero additional cost" — revenue still flows through the per-shift commissions ([curamcare.com/business](https://www.curamcare.com/business)).
  - **Managed Care (CQC-regulated) service:** a fully managed, regulated care line with care plans and a Registered Manager — pricing not published ([managed-care](https://www.curamcare.com/home-care-services/managed-care)). Hybrid hedge on top of the marketplace.
- **Marketplace vs managed:** primarily introduction/marketplace; the managed arm is a newer, smaller add-on and the vehicle for their CQC registration.

## 2. Pricing (real numbers)

**For families:**
- Rates are set by carers, negotiable. Platform-quoted averages (Aug 2025): **£20.50/hour** visits, **£120/night** overnight, **£1,200/week** live-in ([how-it-works](https://www.curamcare.com/how-it-works), [carer-information](https://www.curamcare.com/carer-information)).
- **+12% client fee** added to every invoice ([client-information](https://www.curamcare.com/client-information)).
- Marketing claim: clients "pay on average 22% less than the current suggested UK Home Care Association hourly rate" ([homepage](https://www.curamcare.com/)).
- **Cost calculator** at [/cost-home-care](https://www.curamcare.com/cost-home-care): pick care type (hourly / sleeping night / waking night / daily or weekly live-in), hours/week, weeks/year; outputs averages from actual platform rates in the last 6 months. Pricing is transparent, not gated behind a callback — no quote forms or sales calls in the self-serve funnel.
- Minimum shift 1 hour; no minimum contract; carer minimum rate £15/hour ([client-information](https://www.curamcare.com/client-information), [carer-information](https://www.curamcare.com/carer-information)).

**For carers:**
- **No sign-up fee**; profile advertised free ([carers page](https://www.curamcare.com/carers)).
- **15% (incl. VAT) commission** per shift invoice; example from Curam's own material: a £16/hr rate nets the carer £13.60/hr ([blog](https://www.curamcare.com/blogs/everything-you-need-to-know-about-curam)).
- Claimed net averages: **£17.85/hour and £1,020/week** after fees; "25% more than an agency salary" ([carers page](https://www.curamcare.com/carers)).
- Payout: card payments released ~2–3 working days after shift end; Direct Debit adds 5–7 days; LA-funded work may pay in arrears ([carer-information](https://www.curamcare.com/carer-information)).

## 3. Client acquisition

- **SEO (large, deliberate footprint):** programmatic-style service pages (`/home-care-services/live-in-care`, `/hourly-care`, `/overnight-care`, plus condition pages: dementia, Parkinson's, autism, post-operative, respite, domiciliary), a `/our-locations` coverage map (location landing pages), a cost calculator page targeting "cost of home care" queries, and a substantial blog (guides like "Working as a self-employed carer", "UK carer marketplace") targeting both demand and supply keywords ([site navigation](https://www.curamcare.com/)). They rank on page 1 for their own category terms; exact organic size not measured here (inferred: large by category standards, given the page architecture).
- **B2B/partnership channel — a major differentiator:** works with **25+ local authorities** directly, and via a **July 2024 Capita partnership** claims reach into **100+ local authorities** for Direct Payment users; NHS discharge teams and Personal Health Budget holders; care homes/domiciliary agencies using Curam as a staffing tap ("arrange care within 20 minutes"); case managers; charity partnerships (Alzheimer's Society, Parkinson's UK named) ([curamcare.com/business](https://www.curamcare.com/business), [healthtechdigital.com Capita story](https://www.healthtechdigital.com/home-care-professional-marketplace-curam-partners-with-capita-to-expand-tech-led-social-care-solutions-for-local-authorities/)).
- **Other partnerships listed:** National Pharmacy Association, Capita, Pillsorted, AgeSpace UK; memberships in case-management bodies (BABICM, CMSUK, IRCM) and TechUK — credibility plays into the complex-care/case-manager referral channel ([about-us](https://www.curamcare.com/about-us)).
- **PR:** startup-press coverage (Maddyness 2024, Private Equity Wire, healthtech trade press) rather than mass consumer PR ([maddyness.com](https://www.maddyness.com/uk/2024/03/19/curam-the-startup-ensuring-that-no-one-goes-without-care/)).
- **Paid ads / referral schemes:** no evidence found of a formal client referral scheme; paid search activity not verified (uncertain — would need ad-library/SEM tooling).
- **Trust/review platforms:** actively farms Trustpilot ("Rated Excellent", 500–600+ reviews) and republishes reviews as SEO blog content ([blogs/client-trustpilot-reviews](https://www.curamcare.com/blogs/client-trustpilot-reviews)).

## 4. Carer supply side

- **Pitch:** "Earn more. Work flexibly." Set your own rates, choose your hours, ~25% more take-home than agency work, free insurance, free profile advertising, "hundreds of jobs posted each week", build a "Micro Team" with other carers for cover ([carers page](https://www.curamcare.com/carers)).
- **Vetting bar (high, and marketed hard):** 21+, right to work, **2 years' UK care experience**, **enhanced DBS/PVG/AccessNI on the update service**, **Care Certificate dated within 3 years**, **2 UK work references** (not friends/family), photo ID, biometric face-scan ID verification "to banking standards", plus a live online approval meeting ([carer-information](https://www.curamcare.com/carer-information), [client-information](https://www.curamcare.com/client-information)).
- **Selectivity as trust signal:** claims only **1 in 7 applicants approved (~14%)**, 11,000+ approved carers with 50,000 in pipeline, average **10 years' experience** ([about-us](https://www.curamcare.com/about-us)).
- **Onboarding friction:** app download is mandatory; 3 steps (download app → complete profile → online approval meeting). Requiring an existing Care Certificate + DBS on the update service pushes cost/effort onto the carer pre-approval — filters for committed professionals but excludes newcomers.
- **Insurance:** £1M public liability + £1M professional liability + legal expenses via CFC, auto-activated on first invoice, covering platform-transacted hours only — a key carrot and a lock-in against off-platform work ([carer-information](https://www.curamcare.com/carer-information)).
- **Supply liquidity claim:** "average of six applicants per post within 24 hours" ([blogs/curam-unveils-real-time-carer-hiring...](https://www.curamcare.com/blogs/curam-unveils-real-time-carer-hiring-and-job-portal-for-agencies-and-care-homes)).

## 5. Website / product teardown

- **Positioning:** "Trusted live-in, hourly & private carers" — cheaper than agencies (−22% vs UKHCA rate), better-paid carers (+25%), safer than Gumtree-style DIY (vetting). The "virtuous circle" framing: cut out agency overhead, split the margin between family and carer ([homepage](https://www.curamcare.com/), [/business](https://www.curamcare.com/business)).
- **Funnel (two entry paths):**
  1. **Post a job:** short questionnaire (needs, schedule, location) → broadcast in real time to local carers → carers apply into the family's account.
  2. **Browse:** postcode search → filter carers by experience, skills, availability, rate, location → view profiles with reviews.
  Then: in-platform messaging + **CuramMeet** video calls → interview/check references → carer issues a **service agreement + shift invoice** → family approves in-app → pays via **Mangopay** (card/bank) → funds held until shift completion, released ~2 days later ([how-it-works](https://www.curamcare.com/how-it-works)). Claim: "95% of clients successfully find care."
- **Trust signals:** Trustpilot "Excellent" badge, DBS/biometric-ID/2-references vetting explained repeatedly, £1M+£1M insurance, CQC "Good" rating (for the managed arm), escrow-style payment holding, phone/email/chat support prominently displayed (01387 730 766 — human fallback for an ageing demographic).
- **Notable product features:** CuramMeet video calls; carer-created service agreements and invoicing in-app; payment escrow via Mangopay; "Family Circle" (share care updates with relatives); favourites + notifications; carer "Micro Teams"; real-time hiring portal for agencies/care homes (B2B SaaS-ish surface).
- **Matching:** no evidence of algorithmic auto-matching; it's broadcast-and-apply plus filtered search. Human support team assists (reviews praise staff helping find carers).

## 6. Regulatory stance

- **Hybrid:** the marketplace itself operates as an introduction platform (families are the hiring party; carers self-employed), but Curam also holds a **CQC registration rated "Good"** and sells a regulated **Managed Care** package with care plans, risk assessments and a Registered Manager ([client-information](https://www.curamcare.com/client-information), [managed-care](https://www.curamcare.com/home-care-services/managed-care)).
- Presentation: the CQC badge is used site-wide for trust, while FAQs make clear clients "may opt for non-regulated packages" (the default self-serve product). Negative Trustpilot reviews show the flip side: when things go wrong on the self-serve side, Curam positions itself as "merely an introductory company," which frustrates some clients ([Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/curamcare.com)).

## 8. Strengths, weaknesses, lessons for CareMatch

**Strengths**
- Dual-sided monetisation (~24% blended take) with pricing that still undercuts agencies on both sides — the "virtuous circle" story is simple and true.
- High, loudly-marketed vetting bar (1-in-7 approval, biometric ID, DBS update service) converts a compliance cost into the core trust asset.
- B2B distribution moat: 25+ direct LA relationships, Capita reach into 100+ councils, NHS discharge, case managers, charities — repeat, bulk demand that pure-consumer rivals lack.
- Full transaction rails (agreements, invoicing, Mangopay escrow, insurance tied to platform hours, £1,000 circumvention fee) make staying on-platform rational for both sides.
- CQC-registered managed arm de-risks the "introduction agency takes no responsibility" objection and unlocks regulated-funding clients.

**Weaknesses**
- Introduction-model service gap: reviews cite carer no-shows, churn ("5 carers in 5 months"), and Curam disclaiming responsibility — reliability is the model's soft underbelly.
- App-mandatory onboarding and Care Certificate/DBS prerequisites throttle supply growth (50k pipeline vs 11k approved).
- Fee opacity at first touch: 12% client fee surfaces in FAQs/invoices rather than headline pricing; some reviewers report surprise charges.
- Modest funding (~£5M) for a "UK's largest" claim — thin war chest; growth leans on partnerships and SEO, not brand advertising.

**Lessons for CareMatch**
1. **Copy the dual-sided fee structure but make it headline-transparent.** ~15% carer + ~12% client is the proven benchmark; CareMatch can differentiate by showing the all-in client price on every profile and in the calculator, pre-empting Curam's fee-surprise complaints.
2. **Build the anti-disintermediation bundle from day one:** insurance that only covers platform-booked hours + escrow payments + contract/invoice tooling + explicit circumvention fee. Curam proves carrots (insurance, fast payout) plus one stick (£1,000 fee) is enough.
3. **Treat vetting selectivity as marketing, not overhead.** Publish the approval rate, experience averages, and each check on carer profiles — Curam's "1 in 7" and "10 years average experience" lines do heavy conversion lifting.
4. **Attack the reliability gap.** Curam's worst reviews are continuity failures. Features like backup-carer pools (their "Micro Teams" is a half-step), guaranteed-cover promises, or a light managed tier would out-position them where they're weakest.
5. **Don't ignore B2B demand.** Direct Payments / PHB holders via councils and NHS discharge are Curam's scale engine (100+ LAs via Capita). Even pre-launch, a self-serve product should be architected so a council Direct-Payment team can post jobs and get consolidated billing — it's the same product with an org account layer.

**Key open questions / uncertainties:** exact revenue and unit economics (small-company accounts, nothing filed publicly); paid-acquisition spend; whether the homepage's "10% platform fee" reference (seen in one fetch) reflects a promo or an error — the documented fees are 15% carer / 12% client; true core headcount.
