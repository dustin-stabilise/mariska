# Helping Hands Home Care — Competitive Intelligence Report

**TL;DR:** Helping Hands is the national *fully-managed* home-care incumbent: founded 1989, CQC/CIW-regulated, 155+ branches, ~5,900 direct-employed staff, £175m turnover (FY2024). It charges families from **£32.40/hr + £4.50/visit travel** and **from £1,675/week live-in**, while paying visiting carers roughly £11–13/hr and live-in carers "up to £675/week" — an effective gross take on live-in of roughly 55–60% of the family's spend (computed, see §2). Its marketing explicitly attacks the introduction-agency model on regulation, carer-replacement, and payroll-hassle grounds. It defines both the price umbrella CareMatch undercuts and the objection script CareMatch must answer.

---

## 1. Business model

- **Fully-managed, CQC-registered domiciliary care provider** — the opposite pole from an introduction agency. Families pay Helping Hands; Helping Hands directly employs the carers ("we directly employ all our carers and never use agency staff" — [homepage](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/)). Legal entity: Midshires Care Limited, Company No. 3959933 ([why-choose page](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/about-us/why-choose-helping-hands/), [Companies House](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/03959933)).
- **Revenue streams:** (1) private-pay hourly visiting care, (2) private-pay live-in care (their flagship — they claim to be one of the largest live-in providers in England & Wales), (3) respite/short-term care, (4) local-authority and NHS-funded care (they accept direct payments, personal budgets, CHC funding — [visiting-care costs page](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/costs-funding/costs-of-visiting-care/)).
- **Not a marketplace.** There is no carer choice, browsing, or bidding. The company assigns and manages carers; the family buys an outcome, not a person.
- **Effective take (computed, inferred):** live-in billed from £1,675/wk vs. employed live-in carer pay "up to £675/wk" → carer receives ~40% of family spend; Helping Hands' gross share ≈ 55–60% before employer NI/pension/training costs. Hourly: £32.40/hr billed vs. ~£11–13/hr carer pay (Glassdoor estimate ~£11/hr for visiting carers — [Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.co.uk/Hourly-Pay/Helping-Hands-Home-Care-Hourly-Pay-E763600.htm)) → ~60% gross take. FY2024 accounts show gross profit £85m on £175m turnover (~48% gross margin) and EBITDA £25m (~14%) ([Companies House data via GlobalDatabase](https://uk.globaldatabase.com/company/midshires-care-limited)) — i.e., roughly half of what families pay never reaches carers, and most of that is absorbed by branch/management overhead, not profit.

## 2. Pricing (what families pay / what carers get)

**Families:**
| Service | Price | Source |
|---|---|---|

| Travel charge | **£4.50 per visit** | same page |
| Live-in care | **from £1,675/week** single; **£2,125/week couple** (couple figure from search snippet of the same page — treat as indicative) | [cost-of-live-in-care](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/costs-funding/cost-of-live-in-care/) |
| Respite live-in | **£345/day** (search snippet; indicative) | same page |
| Initial assessment | Free (home, hospital, or care-home visit) | [homepage](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/) |

- Pricing is published openly — notable for a managed provider — and framed as "transparent pricing plans, with all costs of care included… no hidden charges or transaction fees." They benchmark against care homes ("£1,000–£1,500/week for a private room") and the general live-in market ("£900 and £1,800 per week").
- Trustpilot complaints reveal contract friction not shown on pricing pages: reviewers cite **48-hour cancellation charges** and a **2-week paid notice period** on ending care ([Trustpilot via search](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/helpinghandshomecare.co.uk)).
- Note: the earlier ~£1,599/week figure in our brief is stale; current published floor is £1,675/week.

**Carers (employees):**
- Live-in: "competitive salary of **up to £675 per week**" + free food/accommodation on placement, 28 days holiday, pension, statutory sick pay; paid monthly ([live-in salary page](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/jobs/live-in-carer-jobs/live-in-carer-salary-benefits/)). Their own FAQ material concedes self-employed carers elsewhere can earn "up to £800/week… but without benefit contributions" (search snippet of [live-in FAQ](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/jobs/live-in-carer-jobs/live-in-carer-faqs/)).
- Visiting: exact rates hidden ("above the market rate", "generous mileage allowance") — pushed to the jobs portal ([pay & benefits page](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/jobs/care-assistant-jobs/pay-benefits-care-assistant/)). Glassdoor estimates ~£11/hr / ~£23.6k for a visiting carer.
- **The spread is the story:** family pays £32.40/hr; carer sees ~£11–13/hr. On live-in, family pays £1,675+/wk; carer sees ≤£675/wk. This is the gap an introduction model monetises differently.

## 3. Client acquisition

- **SEO is the primary engine — a huge programmatic footprint.** ~155+ branch location pages (`/our-locations/{town}/`), each with sub-pages per service (`/our-locations/cardiff/live-in-care/`) and per-location cost pages (`/our-locations/leicester/cost-of-care/`) plus local blogs ("How much does home care cost in Kent"). On top: a `/costs-funding/` hub with query-matching money pages ("Cost of Live-In Care: UK Prices & 2026 Guide", cost of personal care, elderly care, assisted living, community care, adult care) and condition-led service pages (dementia, stroke, Parkinson's, MND, palliative). They transparently publish prices partly *because* cost queries are the highest-intent keywords. (Observed across search results, e.g. [costs-funding hub](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/costs-funding/).)
- **Comparison/objection content:** dedicated pages targeting the introduction-agency comparison — [Introductory vs fully managed live-in care providers](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/live-in-care/introductory-vs-fully-managed-live-in-care-providers/) and [Introductory v fully managed visiting care](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/visiting-care/fully-managed-visiting-care/) — designed to intercept families considering Elder/Curam/Country Cousins.
- **Phone-first conversion:** national number 0330 037 6958 staffed 7 days, callback forms, brochure requests, free assessments. No online booking or self-serve.
- **Directories/partnerships:** listed on [homecare.co.uk](https://www.homecare.co.uk/homecare/agency.cfm/id/65432224908) and the [NHS service directory](https://www.nhs.uk/services/care-provider/helping-hands-homecare-services/X58441); hospital-discharge content ([after-hospital care](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/home-care-services/after-hospital-care/)) and acceptance of council direct payments/CHC funding position them for statutory referral flows. Specific council contracts not verifiable from public sources (inferred from funding pages + scale).
- **Brand/social proof:** Trustpilot badge (5,050+ reviews, 4.5★) on every page; "36 years" heritage; awards page. A [3Search case study](https://www.3searchgroup.com/resources/casestudy/helping-hands-home-care/) shows they built out a professional sales & marketing function under PE ownership (Livingbridge introduction). Paid search likely but not directly verified.

## 4. Carer supply side

- **Pitch:** employment security vs. gig work — "No franchises. No agencies. No tax returns"; paid holidays (28 days), pension, sick pay, 24-hour carer helpline, free 4-day training, career progression, free uniform ([live-in jobs](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/jobs/live-in-carer-jobs/), [care-assistant jobs](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/jobs/care-assistant-jobs/)).
- **Vetting:** application + interview, minimum two references, enhanced DBS, regional assessment centre, then a 4-day Care Certificate-aligned training programme before unsupervised work; claim to hire "only the kindest, most dedicated 2% of applicants" (marketing claim, unverifiable) ([carer training process](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/jobs/carer-training-process/)).
- **Onboarding friction is high by design** (weeks: DBS + references + assessment day + training) — a moat for quality claims but a recruiting bottleneck. Separate jobs portal (jobs.helpinghands.co.uk).
- **Economics for carers are the weak flank:** their own materials admit self-employed live-in carers can out-earn their employed ones (£800 vs £675/wk). Glassdoor/Indeed put visiting pay near the sector floor (~£11–13/hr). ~5,939 employees, +4% YoY ([accounts data](https://uk.globaldatabase.com/company/midshires-care-limited)).

## 5. Website / product teardown

- **Positioning:** "Live well in the home you love." Care "tailored to you" from 30-minute visits to 24-hour live-in. Emotional, reassurance-led, targets the adult-child decision-maker.
- **Funnel:** enquiry (phone/callback) → free in-person assessment by local care manager (home/hospital/care home) → tailored care plan → "care in as little as 24 hours" → assigned matched carer ([visiting care page](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/visiting-care/)). Human-driven end to end; **zero self-serve product** — no carer profiles, no online matching, no messaging, no online payments visible. "Matching" is a manual branch-manager function ("a carer… matched to your needs and interests").
- **Trust signals:** CQC ID 1-101671690 and CIW registration displayed; per-branch CQC ratings (spot-checked branches rated "Good" — [CQC](https://www.cqc.org.uk/provider/1-101671690)); Trustpilot 4.5★/5,050+; 36 years; 10,000+ customers monthly; 155+ branches; direct-employment guarantee; free assessment; 24-hour start promise.
- **Weaknesses in the experience:** no pricing calculator, no carer visibility before contract, phone-gated everything, and per-branch quality variance shows up in reviews (inconsistent carers, late arrivals, surprise cancellation/notice fees — [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/helpinghandshomecare.co.uk)).

## 6. Regulatory stance

- Fully **CQC-registered (England) and CIW-registered (Wales)** provider; regulation is their #1 marketing weapon. They explicitly frame introduction agencies as a risk: "Many other home care providers act as introductory agencies, and as such are not regulated at all" ([why-choose](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/about-us/why-choose-helping-hands/)).
- Their anti-introductory script (from [the comparison page](https://www.helpinghandshomecare.co.uk/live-in-care/introductory-vs-fully-managed-live-in-care-providers/)): with an introductory agency **you** hold the contract and are "responsible for their pay"; **you** must "find a replacement" if the carer is ill; "the amount you pay could change on a daily basis"; carers are "not regularly monitored"; agencies are "unregulated by the CQC and CIW." They concede introductory is "slightly cheaper." This is precisely the objection list CareMatch's product and copy must neutralise.

## 8. Strengths, weaknesses, lessons for CareMatch

**Strengths:** national brand + 36-year heritage; CQC regulation as a trust monopoly weapon; transparent published pricing; enormous local-SEO moat; 24-hour start-of-care promise; full accountability (replacements, payroll, supervision) that genuinely removes family burden.

**Weaknesses:** ~50%+ of family spend absorbed above the carer; carers paid near sector floor (their own pages admit self-employed carers earn more); phone-gated, zero self-serve product; branch cost base caps price flexibility; per-branch quality variance and fee-surprise complaints; no carer choice for families.

**Lessons for CareMatch:**
1. **Price against the £1,675/wk and £32.40/hr umbrella explicitly.** Show the maths on a comparison page: "family pays £1,675, the carer sees ≤£675 — with CareMatch the carer keeps X%, you pay Y." Their published prices make this attack easy and citable.
2. **Pre-write answers to their five objections** (who pays the carer, who covers sickness/holiday, price variability, monitoring, regulation). Product features — backup-carer pools, escrowed/managed payments, verified DBS + insurance badges, standardised weekly pricing — should each map to one objection, and a "managed vs introduction: the honest comparison" SEO page should target the same keywords their comparison pages own.
3. **Copy the cost-transparency SEO playbook.** Their highest-intent pages are "cost of X care" guides and per-town cost pages. CareMatch can win these with genuinely lower numbers plus a real calculator — something Helping Hands can't publish without exposing its margin.
4. **Recruit carers on the pay gap.** "Keep more of the £32/hr families pay" beats "£11/hr plus a uniform." Their 4-day training + assessment-centre funnel is slow; a fast, verification-first onboarding with transparent earnings is a supply-side wedge — but must not compromise on enhanced DBS + references, since that's the trust bar they've set.
5. **Match their speed promise.** "Care in as little as 24 hours" is a benchmark; an introduction platform must show live carer availability and time-to-first-match, or the managed incumbent wins urgent (hospital-discharge) demand by default.

*Uncertainty notes: couple's live-in rate (£2,125/wk) and £345/day respite came from search snippets of their pricing page rather than a direct page fetch; FY2024 financials are a third-party summary of Companies House filings; "2% of applicants" and "top of market pay" are unverified marketing claims; paid-advertising spend and council contract specifics are inferred, not confirmed.*
