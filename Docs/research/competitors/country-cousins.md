# Country Cousins — Competitive Intelligence Report

**One-liner:** The UK's longest-established (1959) introductory live-in care agency — a phone-led, human-matched introduction service connecting families with ~1,500 self-employed live-in carers nationwide, now a brand within PE-owned Trinity Homecare Group.

## 1. Business model

- **Model:** Classic **introduction agency** (not a managed provider, not CQC-registered). Country Cousins matches families with self-employed live-in carers; the family contracts with and pays the carer directly. Homepage explicitly states carers are "Self-employed, contracted directly by you" ([homepage](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/)).
- **Two revenue streams from families** (families pay everything; carers appear to pay nothing to the agency):
  1. **Ongoing agency fee** — charged per day of care for "matching clients with suitable and trusted live-in carers on an ongoing basis", from **£37.50/day** ([cost page](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/live-in-care/cost-of-live-in-care/), surfaced via search snippets — page body hides figures behind "pricing available on request").
  2. **One-off lifetime registration fee** — **£280** covering consultation, requirements setup and first match (same source; a Trustpilot reviewer mentions paying a £200 registration fee, so the figure has moved over time — inferred).
- **Carer payment flow:** "carers are self-employed, they will invoice you directly for their services, and you are responsible for paying the carer at the end of each week" ([cost page](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/live-in-care/cost-of-live-in-care/)). So the agency does **not** intermediate payments — no payments product at all.
- **Implied take rate:** with a recommended carer rate from **£118/day** plus **£37.50/day** agency fee, the agency captures roughly **24% of the total daily spend** (£37.50 / £155.50) on an ongoing basis — a very healthy effective commission for a pure-introduction service, charged to the family rather than deducted from the carer. (Arithmetic inferred from published figures.)
- **Group structure / dual-brand play:** Country Cousins (introductory, unregulated) sits alongside **Trinity Homecare** (fully managed, CQC-rated "Outstanding") and **Patricia White's** (premium introductory) in Trinity Homecare Group. The homepage openly cross-sells: introductory care for families "confident in managing care arrangements themselves"; Trinity for those wanting "minimal admin, guaranteed cover and regulated oversight" ([homepage](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/)). One lead-gen engine, two monetisation paths.

## 2. Pricing (verbatim numbers)

**Families:**
- Headline: live-in care "starts at around £1,000 [a week] or £4,000 a month" vs care home "approximately £1,250 a week" ([cost of live-in care](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/live-in-care/cost-of-live-in-care/)).
- Recommended carer rate: **from £118/day** (~£826/week, paid direct to carer).
- Agency fee: **from £37.50/day** (~£262.50/week to Country Cousins).
- Registration: **£280 one-off lifetime fee**.
- Three tiers: Standard live-in care / Advanced live-in care / Night support; bank-holiday rates "vary" (carers elsewhere say double pay).
- Extras borne by the family: carer's **food and household utilities**, **travel** ("second-class return rail ticket or current HMRC mileage rates"), **waking-night fees** (£12 per single night call, up to £24 for multiple calls per carer FAQ).
- **Funnel behaviour when pricing is hidden:** the cost page says "pricing is available on request. If you would like a digital or printed copy of our pricing structure, please contact us directly" — i.e. the exact rate card is a phone-call conversion device, while blog/FAQ content leaks the anchor numbers for SEO.

**Carers:**
- Earn **£103–£142/day**, "up to £996 per week" before tax, "up to £4,300 in a single month"; **double pay on bank holidays**; free room and board on assignment; travel expenses covered ([carer FAQs](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/care-jobs/live-in-carer-faqs/), [why join](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/care-jobs/why-join-country-cousins/)).
- No evidence of any carer-side fee, commission or subscription — the agency fee is entirely client-side.
- **£250 cash carer-referral bonus** for recommending a new carer.

## 3. Client acquisition

- **Phone-first funnel:** dominant CTA is "Call us today on 01293 224706" (plus an 0808 freephone) with long opening hours (Mon–Fri 9–6, weekends 9–5) and an explicit "average call wait time is under one minute" promise. Online enquiry form exists but is secondary ([live-in care page](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/live-in-care/)).
- **SEO is the visible growth engine:**
  - WordPress + Yoast, 8 sub-sitemaps; **three location sitemaps** with 1,000+ URLs each visible — a large programmatic footprint of `areas-we-cover/home-care-[county]/[town]/` pages covering counties down to village level (e.g. Grantchester, Burnham Market), mostly last modified Sept 2024 ([sitemap](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/sitemap.xml), [location sitemap](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/location-sitemap.xml)). Likely 2,000–3,000+ location pages total (inferred).
  - Cost-intent content targeting "how much do carers cost", "cost of live-in care", "live-in care FAQs", "guide to the cost of care" — these pages rank and carry the real numbers the sales pages hide.
  - Type-of-care landing pages (dementia, companion, couples, elderly, respite) and case studies.
- **Partnerships:** a public partner directory — SOLLA (later-life financial advisers), Age Space, Jelly Drops, Driving Miss Daisy, Kinly, Good Life Sorted, Empower Rehab, Mobility Hire, CEDA Healthcare (private GP), Age Care Bathrooms, etc. ([partnerships](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/partnerships/)). No public referral-fee terms. Notably **no visible council/NHS/hospital-discharge contracting** — consistent with a private-pay introduction model; they do appear in council resource directories (e.g. Warwickshire SearchOut) as a listed service.
- **Reviews as acquisition asset:** Feefo integration (with a tree-planted-per-review gimmick) plus Trustpilot; review pages and badges woven through the site.
- **Speed as differentiator:** "care in place within 24 hours" / carer availability "within 48 hours" — urgency messaging aimed at hospital-discharge/crisis searches.
- No evidence found of significant paid-ads spend or PR beyond blog content (uncertain — not directly measurable with tools used).

## 4. Carer supply side

- Scale claim: "over 1,500 live-in carers" nationwide ([why join](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/care-jobs/why-join-country-cousins/)).
- **Vetting bar (for an introduction agency, fairly substantive):** UK right-to-work, enhanced DBS/PVG, **minimum 6 months' experience in a regulated care environment**, **two professional references** (one from current/previous employer), training certificates, proof of address; face-to-face or virtual interview + induction ([carer FAQs](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/care-jobs/live-in-carer-faqs/)). Site also claims carers are trained in safeguarding, moving & handling, and life support.
- **Carer pitch:** flexibility ("choice of when you work"), assignment lengths typically **2–6 weeks** rotations, free board, defined working limits ("maximum 10 hours care daily plus 2-hour break"), dedicated support staff, £250 refer-a-friend.
- **Onboarding friction:** moderate — document collection, interview, DBS, references; no carer fees. Recruitment runs through their own jobs section and third-party job boards (liveincarejobs.co.uk, Trinity group careers site — [example ad](https://jobs.trinityhomecaregroup.co.uk/vacancies/37/live-in-carer--self-employed.html)).
- **Quality risk showing in reviews:** Trustpilot complaints allege thin carer profiles ("offered just one 'CV'… no dates, no names of organisations or employers, no references" shown to the family) and occasional placement failures ([Trustpilot](https://uk.trustpilot.com/review/country-cousins.co.uk)) — vetting exists but is opaque to the client at decision time.

## 5. Website / product teardown

- **Positioning:** "Care where you're happiest. Home." + heritage ("the UK's longest-serving introductory live-in care provider", "over 60 years' experience") + speed (24-hour placement) + scale ("Over 400 families trust us every single day").
- **Conversion funnel:** 3 steps — **Call → Register (assessment) → Begin care**. Matching is done by human "Care Relationship Executives" considering needs, location, and shared interests (cooking, gardening, pets); the family gets "a say in choosing your live-in carer" with an introduction call before commitment ([live-in care](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/live-in-care/)). **There is no online matching product** — no carer search, no profiles, no messaging, no online booking, no payments. The website is a lead-capture brochure for a telephone operation.
- **Trust signals:** 60+ years heritage; Feefo 4.5/5 (~122 reviews shown); Trustpilot "Excellent" 4.5/5; sister-brand CQC "Outstanding" halo ("top 4% of registered care providers in England"); partner logos; named case studies; vetting/training claims. No money-back guarantee found.
- **Notable gaps (CareMatch's opening):** no self-serve carer browsing, no transparent rate card on-page, no online contracting/e-signature, no payment handling, no care-plan or scheduling tooling — the family absorbs all employer-style admin after introduction.

## 6. Regulatory stance

- **Introduction agency, explicitly not CQC-registered.** The site is unusually candid: it presents a comparison of "Introductory Care" (self-employed carers, client manages arrangements, not CQC-registered) vs "Fully Managed Care" via Trinity Homecare (CQC-registered, "Outstanding") and routes complex/personal-care and "want regulated oversight" prospects to the sister brand ([homepage](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/)).
- This dual-brand structure is itself the regulatory strategy: the group keeps unregulated-introduction economics while retaining a compliant destination for leads that need regulated care — and uses the CQC rating as borrowed trust for the unregulated brand.

## 7. Traction & financials

- **Founded 1959** by Josephine McGregor (ex-Royal Navy Nursing Sister) as "Country Cousins and Emergency Mothers" in West Sussex ([about](https://www.country-cousins.co.uk/about/)).
- **Ownership journey:** long-independent → acquired into **Saga plc** (Saga Healthcare) → **March 2020 carve-out MBO backed by Limerston Capital**, combining Country Cousins + Patricia White's with Trinity Homecare to form Trinity Homecare Group ([Private Equity Wire](https://www.privateequitywire.co.uk/limerston-capital-partners-acquires-trinity-homecare-group-limited/), [Limerston portfolio page](https://www.limerstoncap.com/portfolio/trinity-homecare)). Limerston added Berkeley Home Health in 2021, "more than doubling the group's EBITDA" (Limerston's own claim).
- **Scale signals:** "over 500 clients each week" (Trinity about page) / "over 400 families every single day" (CC site); ~1,500 self-employed carers. At ~£262.50/week agency fee × 400–500 active clients, Country Cousins' brand-level introduction revenue is plausibly in the **£5.5–7m/year** range (inferred estimate, not a published figure).
- Group entities: Trinity Homecare Group Ltd (08023909), Trinity Homecare Holdings Ltd (12405502), Country Cousins Homecare Agencies Ltd (12087784) — accounts filed to 31 Mar 2025; specific turnover not retrieved ([Companies House](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/08023909)).
- **Reviews:** Trustpilot "Excellent" 4.5/5 (~6 pages, roughly 100–120 reviews — modest volume for 60 years of trading); Feefo 4.5/5 with only ~21 ratings in the past year. Growth trajectory unclear; PE ownership plus recent site investment (2024 location-page refresh) suggests stable, not explosive (inferred).

## 8. Strengths, weaknesses, lessons for CareMatch

**Strengths**
- 60+ years of brand trust and the "longest-established" claim — unassailable heritage positioning.
- Rich per-day economics (~24% effective ongoing take, client-paid) without touching payments, payroll or CQC compliance.
- Speed promise (24–48h placement) backed by a 1,500-carer bench.
- Dual-brand regulatory hedge with a CQC-Outstanding sister to cross-sell and borrow trust from.
- Large programmatic local-SEO estate feeding a high-touch phone sales team.

**Weaknesses**
- **Zero self-serve product**: phone-only matching, no carer profiles/search, no online payments or contracts — exactly the incumbent behaviour CareMatch can convert online.
- Pricing opacity on sales pages (rate card "on request") creates friction and distrust; their own blog leaks the numbers anyway.
- Vetting opaque to families at decision time (single anonymised "CV" complaints); mixed reviews on admin/communication.
- Ongoing per-day agency fee is hard to justify forever on a pure introduction — churn/disintermediation risk once family and carer know each other (inferred).
- Live-in only — no hourly/visiting care segment under this brand.

**Lessons for CareMatch**
1. **The introduction model is durable and lucratively priced.** 60+ years, PE-owned, ~24% ongoing effective take charged to the family (£37.50/day + £280 sign-up) — CareMatch does not need to race to the bottom on fees; it needs to justify them with visible ongoing value (re-matching, cover, tooling) or risk the same disintermediation critique.
2. **Charge the family, not the carer.** Country Cousins keeps carer supply frictionless (no carer fees, £250 referral bounty, 2–6-week flexible rotations) and monetises the demand side. Carer acquisition is the scarcer resource.
3. **Publish the rate card.** Their "pricing on request" funnel is a conversion tax and a review-complaint magnet; their SEO blog leaks the real numbers regardless. Transparent on-page pricing is a cheap, defensible differentiator against every phone-led incumbent.
4. **Productise the trust layer they keep offline.** Families complain about anonymised CVs and unverifiable references. Showing verified DBS status, named references, training badges and reviews on a carer profile *before* the family commits directly attacks their weakest point.
5. **Copy the SEO playbook, beat the funnel.** Thousands of town-level "home care in X" pages + cost-intent content is their demand engine — replicable with programmatic SEO. But where their pages end in "call us", CareMatch pages should end in live, browsable carer profiles and a self-serve match — converting the same searches at lower cost per acquisition.
6. **Mirror their regulatory candour.** Their explicit "introductory vs fully managed" comparison table shows you can present non-CQC status honestly and still convert; CareMatch should adopt the same plain-English framing (and a referral path for cases needing regulated care).

Revenue estimate and take-rate arithmetic are inferred. Trustpilot review counts approximate (page blocked direct fetch).*
