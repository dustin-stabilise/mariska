# SuperCarers (supercarers.com) — Competitive Intelligence [DEFUNCT]

**TL;DR:** VC-backed London care introduction marketplace (founded 2014/15 by brothers Adam & Daniel Pike). Raised roughly £5m+ (£1.1m crowdfunding 2016 + £3.8m round led by Mobeus in 2018). Charged families ~£16/hr with a 25% commission (20% live-in); carers self-employed, setting own rates, keeping 75–80%. Never got past London/Greater-London liquidity for hourly care; diversified into B2B lead-gen (Vitality, My Family Care, agency referrals) before selling to franchise giant Home Instead in June 2020 for an undisclosed sum. The marketplace was wound down within months; the corporate shell was renamed Oakheath Ltd, put into members' voluntary liquidation (June 2020) and dissolved June 2022. The domain today 301-redirects to homeinstead.co.uk — pure lead-gen for the acquirer. This is the closest precedent to CareMatch's exact model and the most instructive failure in the space.

Sources: [Companies House 09279761](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/09279761) · [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/supercarers) · [FinSMEs funding](https://www.finsmes.com/2018/03/supercarers-raises-3-8m-in-funding.html) · [UKTN](https://www.uktech.news/news/supercarers-raises-3-8m-to-provide-personalised-care-for-those-in-need-20180326) · [Care Home Environment on acquisition](https://www.thecarehomeenvironment.com/story/32852/home-instead-senior-care-acquires-supercarers) · Wayback Machine archives of supercarers.com (2017–2021), fetched via web.archive.org CDX.

## 1. Business model

- **Pure introduction agency ("introductory service")**, deliberately contrasted against managed agencies: "Unlike an agency, which directs and manages the delivery of care, we focus on identifying excellent carers, making personalised matches, and giving you the tools to coordinate care directly with your carer" ([archived /how-it-works/, Nov 2019](https://web.archive.org/web/20191115160000/https://supercarers.com/how-it-works/)).
- **Who pays whom:** the family pays through the platform on a weekly consolidated invoice; SuperCarers deducts commission and passes the rest to the self-employed carer. "Your clients make payments through the SuperCarers platform and you will receive 75% of what the customer pays for day or night care and 80% for live-in care" ([archived carer-pay FAQ](https://web.archive.org/web/20191113024335/https://supercarers.com/faq/carers/payment/how-much/)).
- **Take rate: 25% commission on hourly/night care, 20% on live-in care** (of the total the family pays), disclosed openly: "SuperCarers' commission amounts to 25% for hourly and night-time care, and 20% for live-in care… We are a disclosed agent for VAT purposes and our fee includes VAT" ([archived fees FAQ](https://web.archive.org/web/20191115155541/https://supercarers.com/faq/families/prices/carer-earnings/)). No registration or upfront fees for families ([archived FAQ](https://web.archive.org/web/20191115155855/https://supercarers.com/faq/families/prices/fees/)).
- **Secondary revenue streams (late-stage diversification signals):**
  - **B2B lead-gen to regulated agencies** — an "/agencies/" programme inviting CQC-regulated agencies (e.g. a Right at Home co-branded page) to "receive qualified leads," free to join, priced per job ([archived /agencies/, Jul 2019](https://web.archive.org/web/20190724065150/https://supercarers.com/agencies/)). So by 2019 they were monetising demand they couldn't serve with their own carer pool.
  - **Insurance partnership:** Vitality members got "CareCoach" (expert consultations) and — notably for our naming — a service literally called **"CareMatch"**, plus 20% discount on the first £1,000–£3,000 of care ([Vitality partner page](https://www.vitality.co.uk/rewards/partners/supercarers/)).
  - **Employee benefits:** partnership with My Family Care, "the nation's biggest employee benefits provider… more than 600,000 employees across over 160 big businesses" (archived /agencies/ page).
- Carers were explicitly self-employed; SuperCarers provided timesheets, scheduling, automated payments, and invoicing tooling.

## 2. Pricing (real numbers, 2019)

**Families** ([archived /how-it-works/](https://web.archive.org/web/20191115160000/https://supercarers.com/how-it-works/) and [rates FAQ](https://web.archive.org/web/20191115155931/https://supercarers.com/faq/families/prices/carer-rates/)):
- Day care: **from ~£16/hour** (carer rate + commission combined), **minimum visit 3 hours**.
- Night care: **from £130/night** sleeping (≈£14/hr), **£145/night** waking (≈£16/hr).
- Live-in care: **from £795/week** (positioned as "a more comfortable and affordable alternative to residential care").
- Bank holidays: recommended 1.5× multiplier, auto-applied on the invoice ([archived FAQ](https://web.archive.org/web/20191115155902/https://supercarers.com/faq/families/prices/bank-holiday-rates/)).
- No sign-up fee, no upfront fee; commission baked into the hourly price and itemised on a weekly invoice.

**Carers** ([archived carer-pay FAQ](https://web.archive.org/web/20191113024335/https://supercarers.com/faq/carers/payment/how-much/)):
- Keep **75%** (hourly/night) or **80%** (live-in) of what the client pays.
- Set their own rates, no upper cap; platform "recommended rates" defaulted onto new profiles: **£12/hr for short day shifts, £10.50/hr for 6+ hour shifts, up to £108.75 for a 10-hour waking night**. Recommendations pitched as guaranteeing at least the Real Living Wage.
- Sales pitch to investors/press: carers keep 80% for live-in "vs. <40% with traditional agencies" ([UKTN, Mar 2018](https://www.uktech.news/news/supercarers-raises-3-8m-to-provide-personalised-care-for-those-in-need-20180326)).

**Funnel where pricing wasn't shown:** pricing WAS public (a differentiator — "Pricing: save on unnecessary agency costs" sat in the top nav), but every page pushed a dual CTA: an online "Find your Super Carer" questionnaire (~5 minutes) OR a phone line ("If you'd rather a friendly voice to an online form… Call us on 020 8629 1030"). Separate phone numbers for care-seekers (020 8629 1030) and carers (020 8629 1037) — a telesales/advisor layer behind the marketplace.

## 3. Client acquisition (families)

- **SEO content engine (large):** Wayback CDX shows hundreds of archived URLs: 200+ blog posts (dementia guides, "carer of the month," funding-care explainers, and dozens of hyper-local "accessible days out in Leeds/Bristol/Glasgow/Scarborough" listicles), long-form gated guides (Elderly Care Guide, Dementia Care Guide, Funding Care Guide with email-capture signup pages `/care-guide-signup/`, `/dementia-guide-signup/`), plus a 26-page corona FAQ in 2020.
- **Programmatic local SEO:** ~40 `/home-care/{city}/` landing pages (Birmingham, Bristol, Leeds, Manchester, Edinburgh, Glasgow, many London boroughs) each with `/live-in/` sub-pages — despite hourly care only actually operating in London/Greater London (live-in was nationwide). Classic capture-demand-everywhere, serve-it-somewhere play.
- **Guest posting / content partnerships:** archived UTM-tagged inbound URLs reveal placements on Unforgettable (dementia products), SilverSurfers, AgeSpace, Yours Magazine, Active Minds — elderly-audience content sites.
- **Partnerships:** Vitality insurance (member benefit + discount), My Family Care (employee benefits reaching 600k employees), DigitalHealth.London accelerator (NHS-adjacent credibility: "we're helping them to refine, scale and develop, through greater in-depth knowledge of the NHS" — programme director quote on their own site).
- **PR:** founder-story coverage ([City AM "Meet the Pikes"](https://www.cityam.com/meet-pikes-brothers-gave-up-corporate-jobs-and-first-home/)), London Live TV, Share Radio, funding announcements across tech/health press.
- **Phone-assisted conversion:** "care advisory team" handling inbound calls — high-touch sales on top of the self-serve funnel.
- No evidence found of large-scale paid search; one `fbclid` archived URL suggests at least some Facebook activity (inferred, weak signal).

## 4. Carer supply side

- **Pitch:** "Control. With us, you're self-employed. You can set your own rates, hours and areas you work in. We won't tell you where, when or how to do your job… best pay in the industry" ([archived /care-jobs/](https://web.archive.org/web/20191115160000/https://supercarers.com/care-jobs/)).
- **Vetting (heavy, deliberately so):** identity & right-to-work checks, references, **face-to-face group interviews ("we meet every carer in person")**, enhanced DBS required, **minimum 1 year professional care experience**; claimed **only 5% of applicants accepted** (homepage). Third-party verification tech for documents ([archived vetting FAQ](https://web.archive.org/web/20191113024435/https://supercarers.com/faq/families/safety/how-vet/)).
- **Onboarding = 5 steps:** online application (incl. checking "we have enough demand in your area" — supply throttled to demand), detailed experience questions, in-person induction with professional photo, background checks + references, then profile goes live. High friction by design — quality positioning, but expensive and slow to scale.
- **Matching:** "Our algorithms connect you with the right clients" — carers receive job invitations and choose to apply; families view responses, prices, and profiles, then meet/phone before booking.
- **Perks:** partnered training modules, digital timesheets, automated payments; carer testimonials name-checked on care-jobs page.
- Job-type landing pages (`/care-jobs/dementia/`, `/live-in/`, `/respite/`…) were still being expanded in late 2020 — post-acquisition, supply recruitment continued for Home Instead's live-in ambitions.

## 5. Website / product teardown (as of 2019 peak)

- **Headline:** "Find experienced home carers near you. We connect you to qualified carers and help you choose the one that is right for you." Three-point value prop: choice of hundreds of carers / stay in your own home / "Pay less and your carer earns more" ([archived homepage](https://web.archive.org/web/20191115160000/https://supercarers.com/)).
- **Funnel:** postcode search → ~5-min needs questionnaire ("our 5 minute initial questionnaire is your chance to tell us what's really important to you") → algorithmic shortlist; carers apply to the job → family reviews profiles/prices → encouraged to meet or phone the carer → book via platform tools. Phone advisory fallback at every step.
- **Trust signals:** Trustpilot widget ("1000 happy customers and counting", 5-star, 142 reviews — [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/supercarers.com)); "only 5% make it onto the platform"; enhanced DBS/vetting detail; an **Advisory Board** page (incl. Sir Tom Hughes-Hallett, ex-Marie Curie CEO — [UKTN](https://www.uktech.news/news/supercarers-raises-3-8m-to-provide-personalised-care-for-those-in-need-20180326)); insurance FAQs for both sides; honest CQC-status footer (below).
- **Product features:** matching algorithm, carer profiles with prices, scheduling, digital timesheets, weekly consolidated invoicing, automated payments, recommended-rate defaults, holiday-rate auto-multipliers, live chat, customer-service team.
- **Notable constraint:** 3-hour minimum visit for hourly care — good for carer earnings/relationship quality, but it abandons the enormous "30–60 min pop-in" segment that CQC-registered domiciliary agencies serve.

## 6. Regulatory stance

- Explicit and prominent: **not CQC-registered, and proud to explain why.** Footer on every page: "The Care Quality Commission (CQC) defines companies like SuperCarers as an introductory agency pursuant to the Health & Social Care Act 2008."
- Dedicated FAQ: "No, we are not registered with or regulated by the CQC. The CQC explicitly states that introductory services such as ours are outside the scope of regulation… Service providers must be regulated if they direct and manage care, neither of which SuperCarers does and your carer is not our employee. If you require a fully managed care service, then you should look for the CQC logo" ([archived CQC FAQ](https://web.archive.org/web/20191113030631/https://supercarers.com/faq/families/about/cqc-register/)).
- They even routed demand needing managed care to CQC-rated partners (Vitality CareMatch promised providers "rated good or outstanding by the CQC"). A clean, defensible articulation of the introduction-agency line — worth copying almost verbatim in spirit.

## 7. Traction & financials

- **Timeline:** incorporated 24 Oct 2014 ([Companies House](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/09279761)); launched ~Nov 2015; £1.1m equity crowdfunding 2016; **£3.8m round Mar 2018** (Mobeus £2.1m, JamJar Investments — Innocent founders, Seneca Partners) ([FinSMEs](https://www.finsmes.com/2018/03/supercarers-raises-3-8m-in-funding.html)); Tracxn pegs total raised ≈ **$5.4m** ([Tracxn](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/supercarers/__QzqIpe4iUD-mZZnbFDqSO9Xs6XABk1erzwRh1LdTH6Y/funding-and-investors)) — total is uncertain, roughly £4–6m.
- **Stated ambition at the 2018 raise:** "facilitate over 100,000 hours of care per month" within ~18 months ([Growth Business](https://growthbusiness.co.uk/online-care-platform-supercarers-raises-3-8-million-to-invest-in-growth-18003/)). No evidence this was hit.
- **Actual scale signals:** "1000 happy customers and counting" (2019 homepage); 142 Trustpilot reviews lifetime; hourly care confined to London/Greater London; live-in nationwide. Small-company accounts exemptions at Companies House imply modest headcount/revenue (inferred).
- **Exit:** sold to Home Instead UK (215 franchise offices, 9,000 caregivers, 14,000 clients) on 2 June 2020, undisclosed sum, framed as Home Instead buying live-in-care capability and digital know-how ([Care Home Environment](https://www.thecarehomeenvironment.com/story/32852/home-instead-senior-care-acquires-supercarers); [Crunchbase acquisition](https://www.crunchbase.com/acquisition/home-instead-senior-care-uk-acquires-supercarers--bc36cdfe)).
- **Wind-down:** company renamed **Oakheath Ltd** 15 Jun 2020; **members' voluntary liquidation from 18 Jun 2020**; dissolved 12 Jun 2022 (Companies House). MVL = solvent wind-up, i.e. an asset sale then shell closure — not a crash bankruptcy, but almost certainly not a venture-scale return either (inferred; sale price undisclosed, announced mid-COVID).
- **Post-acquisition decay of the product:** by Dec 2020 the homepage had dropped the marketplace entirely — repositioned as "Care guidance from the experts" (advice + signposting, Vitality portal retained) ([archived Dec 2020 homepage](https://web.archive.org/web/20201215000000/https://supercarers.com/)); by ~2022+ the whole domain 301-redirects to homeinstead.co.uk (verified live 2026-07-05). The marketplace itself survived the acquisition by only months.

## 8. Why it ended (assessment)

No public founder post-mortem was found (marked as a gap; Adam Pike's only public framing is the polite acquisition quote — "delighted with the opportunity to join forces" — [Bdaily](https://bdaily.co.uk/articles/2020/06/05/home-care-company-announces-acquisition-to-enhance-live-in-care-services)). The failure mode must be inferred from the evidence trail, which is fairly loud:

1. **Liquidity never generalised beyond London.** Four-plus years in, hourly care was still London-only while 40 city SEO pages harvested demand they couldn't serve with own-platform supply — hence the pivot to selling those leads to regulated agencies (/agencies/ programme).
2. **CAC vs LTV in a high-churn category.** Home-care customers churn by death, hospitalisation, or care-home admission; a 25% take on ~£16/hr requires long client lifetimes to repay content/PR/phone-advisory acquisition costs. The heavy human advisory layer (phone teams, in-person carer interviews) made this a high-opex "marketplace."
3. **Expensive supply acceptance (5% pass rate, in-person interviews)** — quality moat, but supply acquisition cost scaled linearly with growth.
4. **The economics pushed them upmarket** — 3-hour minimum visits and live-in emphasis (bigger baskets, lower take but higher absolute revenue). That is exactly the segment Home Instead wanted, which explains the acquisition: they bought the live-in playbook + vetted carer pool + digital tooling, not the marketplace business.
5. **COVID timing (June 2020)** likely forced the decision window, though hourly home-care demand actually rose post-COVID — the sale looks like a capital/runway event, not a demand event (inferred).

## 9. Strengths, weaknesses, lessons for CareMatch

**Strengths (worth copying):**
- Cleanest public articulation of the introduction-agency model and CQC stance in the market; radical pricing transparency (commission % published in the FAQ).
- Carer-first economics as marketing: "pay less, your carer earns more" is a two-sided message in one line.
- Serious trust architecture: 5% acceptance, in-person interviews, enhanced DBS, advisory board, Trustpilot 5 stars.
- Diversified demand channels early: insurer benefit (Vitality), employer benefit (My Family Care), content/SEO, phone advisory.

**Weaknesses (what killed it):**
- Marketplace liquidity confined to one metro; national SEO footprint wrote cheques local supply couldn't cash.
- High-touch ops (phone sales, physical interviews, group inductions) meant marketplace margins with agency cost structure.
- 3-hour minimum excluded the highest-frequency demand segment.
- ~£5m was nowhere near enough to solve two-sided liquidity + trust + churn in care; they hit the funding market (2020) with neither profitability nor a growth story.

**Lessons for CareMatch:**
1. **Win one geography completely before touching programmatic local SEO.** SuperCarers' 40-city page network generated leads they had to give away to agencies. Build local density first; expand the SEO footprint only where supply exists — or consciously run an agency-referral monetisation arm for out-of-area demand from day one (it was a workable revenue stream for them).
2. **25%/20% commission, £16/hr, £795/wk live-in, 1.5× bank holidays, 75–80% carer share are field-tested UK reference prices** for this exact model (2019 money; index up for 2026). Their transparency didn't hurt them — hidden pricing is not why competitors beat them.
3. **Keep the ops layer self-serve or die by opex.** Every human step SuperCarers added (phone advisors, face-to-face interviews) improved trust but destroyed marketplace economics. CareMatch's self-serve thesis is right; find trust substitutes (video verification, verified digital DBS, reviews) rather than in-person process.
4. **Design for churn-adjusted LTV.** Expect client lifetimes measured in months; either cheapen acquisition (product-led referral, carer-driven virality) or raise basket (live-in, night care — note their live-in take was LOWER at 20% but revenue per client far higher).
5. **Copy their CQC/introductory-agency language pattern** (plain footer statement + honest FAQ + signpost to regulated providers for managed care). It survived scrutiny and became the standard other introducers (e.g. Curam, HomeTouch) converged on. Also: they already used the name "CareMatch" for their Vitality-branded matching service (~2019–2020) — check trademark/passing-off exposure before committing to the brand.
