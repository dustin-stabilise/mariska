# Lottie (lottie.org) — Competitive Intelligence Report

**One-liner:** Accel-backed UK care *discovery* marketplace ("The People's Care Company") — free for families, monetised via pay-per-qualified-enquiry / pay-per-customer fees from 6,000+ care providers, plus a care-home CRM (Found), an eldercare employee benefit (Seniorcare), and a data product (Care IQ). It dominates care-related organic search and is the strongest UK example of SEO-driven family acquisition converted into B2B lead revenue.

**Important scope note for CareMatch:** Lottie is NOT a carer marketplace. It lists **regulated providers** (care homes, home-care *agencies*, retirement developments) — never self-employed individual carers. Its supply side is B2B sales to providers, not carer recruitment. That makes it an acquisition/monetisation lesson, not a like-for-like operating model.

---

## 1. Business model

- **Who pays whom:** Families pay nothing — "a free service that helps families and retirees find the UK's best care homes and retirement living communities" ([lottie.org](https://lottie.org/)). Care providers pay Lottie for demand. Employers pay for Seniorcare. Providers also pay SaaS fees for Found CRM.
- **Revenue streams (4):**
  1. **Pay Per Qualified Enquiry** — providers are charged per qualified, high-intent enquiry; free to list, "success-based pricing", 90-day cancellation notice ([lottie.org/services-for-care-providers/](https://lottie.org/services-for-care-providers/)).
  2. **Pay Per Customer** — pay only when a referral becomes a customer; *exclusive to providers using Found CRM* (a clever wedge: the closed-loop attribution needed for pay-per-placement requires the provider to adopt Lottie's CRM) ([same page](https://lottie.org/services-for-care-providers/)).
  3. **Found by Lottie (SaaS)** — care-sector CRM (enquiry management, email/SMS marketing, occupancy, finance/invoicing). TechCrunch reported subscriptions "starting at £150/month"; Found was an acquisition (~2022) and powers real-time bed availability on the marketplace ([TechCrunch, Oct 2023](https://techcrunch.com/2023/10/09/care-home-marketplace-lottie-raises-21m/)).
  4. **Seniorcare by Lottie** — B2B employee benefit: care-concierge for employees with elderly dependants, resource centre, care fee discounts/"care vouchers" ([lottie.org/seniorcare/](https://lottie.org/seniorcare/)). Plus **Lottie Media** (photography/videography for care homes) and **Care IQ** (market-intelligence data product) ([Startups.co.uk 100, 2025](https://startups.co.uk/startups-100/2025/lottie-2025/)).
- **Marketplace vs managed:** Pure introduction/discovery layer. Lottie never employs carers, holds no care contract, and takes no cut of ongoing care fees today. TechCrunch (2023) reported an *ambition* to move to "a percentage fee from the duration of each residency booked through the platform" — the Pay Per Customer model is the first step. **Take rate/commission per lead is not published anywhere I could find** — pricing is behind a sales call ("Partner with Lottie" form). Treat any per-lead figure as unknown.

## 2. Pricing

**Families: £0.** No paywall anywhere in the funnel. The site's substitute for pricing pages is *cost transparency content*: Lottie publishes average market rates as SEO/lead-gen content, e.g. on [lottie.org/home-care/](https://lottie.org/home-care/): hourly domiciliary care "£28/hour", live-in care "£1,596/week", overnight waking "£244/night", overnight sleeping "£187/night", and a "£135/week" baseline for 5 hrs/week. Also long-form guides at [lottie.org/fees-funding/care-home-costs/](https://lottie.org/fees-funding/care-home-costs/) and [lottie.org/home-care-services-guides/care-at-home-costs/](https://lottie.org/home-care-services-guides/care-at-home-costs/).

**Providers:** no public rate card. What's known:
- Free to list; charged per qualified enquiry OR per customer (Found users only); 90-day notice to cancel ([services-for-care-providers](https://lottie.org/services-for-care-providers/)).
- Found CRM from **£150/month** ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2023/10/09/care-home-marketplace-lottie-raises-21m/)).
- Seniorcare: enterprise pricing, not public ([lottie.org/seniorcare/](https://lottie.org/seniorcare/)).

**What the funnel does instead of pricing:** three parallel "free shortlist" CTAs (care home / home care / retirement), a needs questionnaire (care type, budget, location, start date), then a human "Care Expert" callback + WhatsApp channel for urgent cases ([lottie.org](https://lottie.org/), [free-care-home-shortlist](https://lottie.org/free-care-home-shortlist/)). Human concierge in the loop = enquiry qualification = what makes the leads billable as "qualified".

## 3. Client acquisition (families)

- **SEO is the engine.** Semrush shows ~55% of lottie.org traffic from Google organic, ~22% direct ([Semrush overview](https://www.semrush.com/website/lottie.org/overview/)). The site is a textbook programmatic-SEO build:
  - **Location pages at scale:** `/care-home/<location>` and `/home-care/<location>` for 40+ cities and 13+ counties (London, Birmingham, Leeds, Berkshire, Essex, Kent…) targeting "care homes in X" / "home care in X".
  - **Cost/funding content:** "Care Home Costs & Care Fees UK Average for 2026", "How Much Is Home Care in 2026?" — annually-refreshed money-keyword pages.
  - **Guides hub** (`/guides-insight/`): dementia, funding, choosing care — top-of-funnel informational capture.
  - Claimed audience: "1 million+ monthly care and retirement seekers" ([provider page](https://lottie.org/services-for-care-providers/)); "over 40,000 match with care every month" / "45K+ monthly care seeker matches" (their own claims — unverified); 500K monthly users claimed at Series A ([TechCrunch, 2023](https://techcrunch.com/2023/10/09/care-home-marketplace-lottie-2raises-21m/)).
- **PR / founder brand:** heavy press logos on-site (BBC, Telegraph, Forbes, The Times); founders Will & Chris Donnelly are prolific on LinkedIn (Chris Donnelly has a very large following — he co-founded creator agency Verb); Startups 100 **#1 UK startup 2025** (#8 in 2023, #2 in 2024) ([Startups.co.uk](https://startups.co.uk/startups-100/2025/lottie-2025/)). They commission surveys ("UK Care Seeker Survey", 1,000 respondents) as PR/link-bait.
- **Institutional referrals:** the provider pitch explicitly advertises "free referrals from NHS discharge teams and social workers" flowing through the platform ([services-for-care-providers](https://lottie.org/services-for-care-providers/)) — i.e., Lottie has made itself a tool that discharge teams use, which is both supply of urgent, high-intent leads and a moat.
- **B2B2C via Seniorcare:** employers distribute Lottie to employees as a benefit — a paid *and* self-reinforcing acquisition channel ([lottie.org/seniorcare/](https://lottie.org/seniorcare/)).
- **Social:** active on TikTok/Instagram/YouTube/Facebook/X (footer links) — care-home "feel good" content aligned with the founders' creator-economy background. Paid search: likely bidding on care terms (unverified — I found no direct evidence of scale).

## 4. Supply side (providers, not carers)

- **Who:** 6,000+ "trusted UK care providers" per the current provider page (Startups profile said 3,000 partners as of early 2025 — the 6,000 figure is Lottie's own, plausibly counting individual homes/branches; treat growth trajectory as directionally true, exact numbers as marketing). National agency brands are showcased on the home-care side: Home Instead (241 branches), Helping Hands (143), Caremark (62) ([lottie.org/home-care/](https://lottie.org/home-care/)).
- **Recruitment:** direct B2B sales ("Partner with Lottie"), free listing to remove friction, then monetise enquiries. Provider portal offers availability toggles, enquiry tracking by month/type, and benchmarking vs. other Lottie partners.
- **Vetting:** "Every care home or home care service listed on Lottie is regulated" ([lottie.org](https://lottie.org/)) — i.e., vetting is largely *inherited from CQC registration* plus internal review ("carefully vetted by our team"). Home-care page cites DBS checks and NVQ/Diploma qualifications as requirements met by listed agencies. Notably, the home-care landing page itself doesn't foreground the CQC brand.
- **What providers earn/keep:** all care fees; they pay Lottie marketing cost per enquiry/customer. **Provider-side grumbles exist:** third-party review content reports providers finding fees confusing, seeing few enquiries after 4 weeks, and one reporting only 1 legitimate lead from a dozen calls ([Compare Caring review](https://comparecaring.com/lottie-review-what-it-is-and-how-it-works/) — page content thin; treat as anecdotal). Classic pay-per-lead tension: lead quality disputes.
- **Onboarding friction:** low (free listing, no lock-in beyond 90-day notice) — deliberately so, since inventory breadth is the SEO product.

## 5. Website / product teardown

- **Positioning:** "The People's Care Company"; "Compare care homes, home care services and retirement properties"; emotional, consumer-brand tone (pastel design, real residents) rather than clinical directory. Anti-"postcode lottery", anti-opacity: they lead with the stat that 44% of care seekers regret their choice ([Startups.co.uk](https://startups.co.uk/startups-100/2025/lottie-2025/)).
- **Funnel:** Postcode search or "free shortlist" wizard → needs questionnaire (care type, budget, location, start date) → **human Care Expert curates a shortlist** and providers with availability contact the family → family chooses. WhatsApp channel for urgent care. So it's *directory + concierge hybrid*: the directory earns the traffic, the concierge qualifies and routes the lead (and justifies "qualified enquiry" billing).
- **Trust signals:** Trustpilot 4.9/5 "Excellent" (~50+ pages of reviews, i.e. roughly 1,000+ reviews; [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/lottie.org)); press logos; "every service listed is regulated"; "free" repeated everywhere; named, photographed Care Experts; Lottie Awards (own industry awards programme — trust-building + provider-relations + PR in one).
- **Product features:** real-time bed availability via Found CRM integration (their genuine data moat — competitors scrape static listings; Lottie sees live occupancy); pricing benchmarking data; provider portal analytics; digitised viewings/assessments/referencing (per TechCrunch); Care IQ market-intelligence. Trustpilot criticism: the intake questionnaire is rigid — "wants to put requests in a standardized box" — real enquiries don't fit neatly.
- **Weak spots observed:** no transparent per-provider pricing for families (only market averages); home-care page doesn't mention CQC explicitly; site speed reported slow by third-party checkers (minor).

## 6. Regulatory stance

- Lottie is an **unregulated intermediary/introducer** — it is not a CQC-registered provider and doesn't need to be, because it never arranges or delivers personal care itself; it introduces families to providers who are themselves regulated. Its public framing is entirely "free service that helps families find care" + "every service listed is regulated" ([lottie.org](https://lottie.org/)). I could not retrieve the full terms of use (the /terms-of-use/ URL serves the homepage shell), so exact liability language is **unverified**.
- Contrast with CareMatch: introducing *self-employed individual carers* (rather than regulated agencies) is a materially different regulatory posture — CareMatch must itself carry the "introduction agency, not a regulated provider" disclosure burden that Lottie sidesteps by only listing CQC/CIW/Care-Inspectorate-regulated entities.

## 8. Strengths, weaknesses, lessons for CareMatch

**Strengths**
1. Free-to-family + pay-per-lead aligns with how care search actually happens (urgent, one-off, price-sensitive families; providers with high LTV per placement willing to pay for demand).
2. Programmatic SEO + cost-transparency content owns the top of the funnel cheaply and compounds.
3. Found CRM = live availability data no scraper-directory can match, *and* the attribution rail for higher-value pay-per-placement pricing.
4. Human concierge converts anonymous traffic into billable "qualified" enquiries and generates 4.9★ Trustpilot social proof.
5. Multiple reinforcing revenue lines (leads, SaaS, employer benefit, data) off one traffic asset.

**Weaknesses**
1. Lead-quality disputes with providers (anecdotal but structural to pay-per-lead); pricing opacity on the provider side breeds distrust.
2. No participation in the ongoing care transaction — monetises the introduction once; revenue per family is capped vs. Elder/Cera-style models.
3. Doesn't serve the "I want a cheaper self-employed carer" segment at all — only regulated agencies (avg £28/hr quoted). That's CareMatch's opening.
4. Marketing numbers are self-reported and shifting (3,000 vs 6,000 partners; 40K vs 45K matches); rigid intake questionnaire criticised by users.
5. Concierge model needs headcount; unit economics of human qualification at 1M monthly users are unproven publicly.

**Lessons for CareMatch**
1. **Publish cost content before you have inventory.** Lottie's "£28/hr home care" / "care home costs 2026" pages are its demand engine. CareMatch's wedge headline is the gap: *self-employed carers at ~£15-20/hr vs £28/hr agency* — build location + cost pages around that comparison from day one.
2. **Charge the supply side per outcome, not the family per search.** Every successful UK care directory keeps the family side free. If CareMatch's revenue model is undecided, Lottie is evidence that "free for families, providers/carers pay on success" clears the trust bar; but expect lead-quality disputes — define "qualified" contractually and show carers the evidence per lead.
3. **Qualified > raw leads, and a human (or AI concierge) is what makes them qualified.** Lottie can bill premium prices because enquiries pass through a Care Expert. A self-serve platform still needs a qualification layer (structured needs assessment, budget, start date, verified phone) before a carer sees the lead.
4. **Own an operational data loop early.** Found CRM's live availability is Lottie's moat. The CareMatch equivalent: real-time carer availability calendars + in-platform booking/payments, so matches reflect reality and outcomes are attributable (enabling pay-per-placement later).
5. **NHS discharge teams and social workers are a free, high-intent channel** — Lottie explicitly routes their referrals to partners. Hospital-discharge and local-authority-adjacent relationships are worth pursuing even pre-scale, and they double as trust signals.
