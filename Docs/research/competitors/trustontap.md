# TrustonTap — Competitive Intelligence Report

**One-liner:** A deliberately regional (Thames Valley / South-East England) introduction marketplace matching families with vetted self-employed carers, positioned as "25% cheaper than traditional agencies," run lean on angel money since 2015 with a concierge (phone-led) matching model rather than open self-serve search.

---

## 1. Business model

- **Model:** Introduction agency / two-sided marketplace. Carers are **self-employed contractors** (sole trader, Ltd or LLP); families contract with them via TrustonTap, which handles "all legal agreements, the billing and the administration" ([carer pay page](https://www.trustontap.com/care-jobs/pay-and-benefits/), [carer FAQ](https://www.trustontap.com/care-jobs/frequently-asked-questions/)).
- **Who pays whom:** Families pay TrustonTap through its "secure online payment system" (direct payment to carers is explicitly prohibited); TrustonTap invoices clients weekly against carer timesheets and pays carers a week later ([pricing page](https://www.trustontap.com/care-services/our-pricing/), [carer FAQ](https://www.trustontap.com/care-jobs/frequently-asked-questions/)).
- **Take rate (double-sided):**
  - **Carer side: 18% commission + VAT.** Their own worked example: a carer charging £25/hr takes home ~£19.60/hr ([carer FAQ](https://www.trustontap.com/care-jobs/frequently-asked-questions/)).
  - **Family side: 5% + VAT "administration fee"** added to care costs ([pricing page](https://www.trustontap.com/care-services/our-pricing/)).
  - Combined effective take is roughly ~23% + VAT of the carer's rate — consistent with Nesta's earlier framing that carers "keep around 80 per cent of their client's fee compared with around 40 per cent via a traditional care agency" ([Nesta ShareLab feature](https://www.nesta.org.uk/feature/sharelab-fund-meet-pioneers/trustontap/)).
- **Revenue streams:** the two-sided commission, plus edge fees: short-term booking fees, late-payment fees, early cancellation fee (within first 6 weeks), <48h cancellation charged in full ([pricing page](https://www.trustontap.com/care-services/our-pricing/)).
- Also serves **local-authority-funded clients** (personal budgets) — Nesta grant explicitly funded expansion "beyond private clients to include local authority partnerships with personal budgets" ([Nesta](https://www.nesta.org.uk/feature/sharelab-fund-meet-pioneers/trustontap/)); carer FAQ guarantees weekly pay "even when third parties fund care."

## 2. Pricing (families and carers)

**Families** (all from [our-pricing](https://www.trustontap.com/care-services/our-pricing/) and [FAQ](https://www.trustontap.com/care-services/frequently-asked-questions/); homepage quotes a slightly narrower £22–£30 band):
- Hourly care: **£20–£30/hr, average £26/hr** — pitched as "25% less than most traditional agencies" (they cite £35–£40/hr agency norms). Travel included; mileage during care billed at 45p/mile.
- Overnight: **sleeping night from £155**, **waking night from £200**.
- Live-in: **from £1,200/week** (client provides bedroom + food; carer gets a formal 2–3h daily break).
- Plus the **5% + VAT admin fee**; no call-out fees; minimum booking **4 hours/week**, minimum visit **1 hour** (no 15/30-min visits).
- Commitments: 2 weeks' notice for changes/cancellation; <48h notice = charged in full; early-cancellation fee inside first 6 weeks.

**Carers** ([pay & benefits](https://www.trustontap.com/care-jobs/pay-and-benefits/), [carer FAQ](https://www.trustontap.com/care-jobs/frequently-asked-questions/)):
- **Set their own rates**, typically £20–£28/hr; marketed as "earn up to £22/hr" take-home and "up to 40% more than with a traditional care agency."
- **18% + VAT commission** is the only disclosed carer-side fee; no sign-up fee mentioned anywhere (absence noted, not confirmed).
- Weekly pay via timesheets on a carer portal; premium rates allowed for weekends/bank holidays.

**Funnel note:** pricing is fully public (rare and notable), but there is **no self-serve browse/checkout**. The [find-a-carer](https://www.trustontap.com/find-a-carer/) page is a lead-gen form (who care is for → postcode → care type → contact details) with a "We've found carers near you" tease; conversion completes by phone/callback (0808 278 1112, [request-callback](https://www.trustontap.com/request-callback/)).

## 3. Client acquisition

- **SEO:** Strong service-line + condition + location architecture: /care-services/* (hourly, overnight, live-in, companionship, respite), /condition-led/* (dementia, Alzheimer's, end-of-life, personal care), and **town-level location pages** across five counties (~65+ towns listed on [our-locations](https://www.trustontap.com/our-locations/)) — classic local programmatic SEO targeting "home care in {town}". Advice hub ([/advice/](https://www.trustontap.com/advice/)) with six categories (home care planning, dementia, carers, financial help, safety, health & wellbeing) targets informational queries. Footprint looks **moderate, locally focused** — not a national content machine (inference from site structure; no third-party traffic data obtained).
- **Council/community partnerships:** Listed multiple times (per-town listings) on Oxfordshire County Council's official **"Live Well Oxfordshire" directory**, which notes referrals come from "Social Workers and other Health Care professionals" as well as self-referral ([Live Well listing](https://livewell.oxfordshire.gov.uk/Services/4374)). Nesta funding explicitly supported moving into **local-authority personal-budget clients** ([Nesta](https://www.nesta.org.uk/feature/sharelab-fund-meet-pioneers/trustontap/)). No evidence found of a formal NHS/hospital-discharge contract (searched; nothing surfaced — mark as *not found*, not disproven).
- **Directories:** Present on third-party care directories: TrustedCare ([listing](https://www.trustedcare.co.uk/home-care/trustontap)), liveincare.org.uk, plus Facebook presence.
- **Reviews as channel:** Homepage leads with "4.9/5 from 1,188 reviews" (source of that figure unclear — likely aggregated/internal; **uncertain**). Trustpilot shows ~4.6/5 from ~135–180 reviews ([Trustpilot](https://uk.trustpilot.com/review/trustontap.com)).
- **PR/awards:** Nesta ShareLab grant (2017), Southern Oxfordshire New Business Competition winner (2017), Oxford entrepreneurship coverage ([EnSpire Oxford interview](https://enspire.ox.ac.uk/article/william-cotton-founder-and-managing-director-of-trustontap-ltd)).
- **Paid ads:** No direct evidence gathered (not verified either way). The 0808 freephone number and callback-first funnel suggest phone-tracked demand channels.

## 4. Carer supply side

- **Recruitment pitch:** autonomy ("choose who you care for and when"), better pay ("up to 40% more than an agency"), local work near home, 1-hour minimum visits (dignity/quality framing vs 15-min agency calls), weekly pay, admin handled for you, dedicated support, local carer community/forums ([pay & benefits](https://www.trustontap.com/care-jobs/pay-and-benefits/), [why work for TrustonTap](https://www.trustontap.com/care-jobs/why-work-for-trustontap/)).
- **Vetting:** individual interview, **DBS check**, ID + right-to-work verification, **two professional references**, training-certificate review; acceptance rate stated as **~5%** in FAQ (homepage says "less than 10%") ([FAQ](https://www.trustontap.com/care-services/frequently-asked-questions/)).
- **Onboarding friction:** must register as self-employed (UTR number, ~10 days), apply via trustontap.com/join/, set availability on a portal; matched to opportunities they can accept/decline ([carer FAQ](https://www.trustontap.com/care-jobs/frequently-asked-questions/)).
- **Earnings/utilisation:** some carers bill 40+ hrs/week, others a few hours; hours "vary by location and availability" — an honest admission that demand density drives carer earnings.
- **Insurance:** platform carries **£5m public liability**; "many carers carry personal liability insurance" (i.e., not universally mandated — a softer stance than some rivals); car-based carers must have appropriate vehicle insurance ([FAQ](https://www.trustontap.com/care-services/frequently-asked-questions/)).
- **Supply scale signal:** "over 200 professional carers throughout Oxfordshire" per the council listing ([Live Well](https://livewell.oxfordshire.gov.uk/Services/4374)) — county-level density, not raw national headcount.
- One Trustpilot criticism theme: carer profile skills not always accurate; some inexperienced carers; occasional <24h carer cancellations ([Trustpilot via search](https://uk.trustpilot.com/review/trustontap.com)).

## 5. Website / product teardown

- **Positioning/headline:** "The care you need, when you need it most" + price anchor ("25% less than most traditional agencies") + consistency promise ("same carer whenever possible") ([homepage](https://www.trustontap.com/)).
- **Conversion funnel:** deliberately **concierge, not self-serve**: lead form or phone call → needs conversation → TrustonTap approaches suitable carers (24–48h typical response) → family receives a **selection of matched carer profiles** → optional video call / meet the carer → book ([how it works](https://www.trustontap.com/care-services/how-it-works/), homepage). **No public carer search/browse** — profiles are shared only after the lead form. This trades marketplace transparency for hand-holding and lead capture.
- **Trust signals:** DBS, two references, ~5% acceptance rate, £5m public liability, 4.9/1,188 review badge, 1-hour minimum visits, dedicated support team, freephone number.
- **Product features:** carer portal (availability, timesheets, payment statements, booking confirmations), "innovative matching process" (marketing language; mechanics undisclosed), online payments only, optional pre-booking video calls. **No visible in-app family messaging, care-plan tooling, or care-log features** (absence on public site; may exist post-signup — uncertain).
- **Notably absent:** instant booking, public carer profiles/rates, app-store presence (none found).

## 6. Regulatory stance

- Explicitly an **"introductory agency" as classified by the CQC — not required to register**, and the FAQ is unusually candid about the consequences: TrustonTap "cannot direct care plan changes, recommend visit frequency increases, or replace care workers for personal care assignments" without client instruction; substitute carers are arranged **only on explicit request** ([FAQ](https://www.trustontap.com/care-services/frequently-asked-questions/)).
- Families are told they avoid Employer's NI because carers are self-employed contractors — leaning into the cost advantage of the model.
- No CQC badge or managed-service claims on site; the compliance line (introduction only, client directs care) is drawn carefully and consistently. This is a good template for how to present introduction-agency status without scaring families.

## 7. Traction & financials

- **Founded:** incorporated **10 Nov 2015** (TRUSTONTAP LTD, company no. 09864667, SIC 88990); trading in the Thames Valley since **summer 2016**; registered at Lovegroves Farm, Long Wittenham, Abingdon; status Active, accounts filed to 31 Dec 2024 ([Companies House](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/09864667), [filing history](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/09864667/filing-history), [EnSpire Oxford](https://enspire.ox.ac.uk/article/william-cotton-founder-and-managing-director-of-trustontap-ltd)).
- **Funding:** founder savings/borrowings + **2 angel investors**, a **Nesta ShareLab grant (2017)** and a local business-competition prize — no VC round found. Companies House shows small share allotments (nominal capital to £17,913 in 2021, £23,167 in 2023, £30,465 in 2024), i.e., **repeated small top-up raises**, not growth rounds ([filing history](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/09864667/filing-history), [EnSpire](https://enspire.ox.ac.uk/article/william-cotton-founder-and-managing-director-of-trustontap-ltd)).
- **Size:** files **total-exemption (small company) accounts** — revenue not public; team page names only **3 people** (William Cotton, co-founder/CEO; Antonia Cotton, director; Emily Glenn, customer service manager) ([our team](https://www.trustontap.com/about-us/our-team/)). Very lean HQ; ops leverage comes from the self-employed carer pool.
- **Growth signals:** expanded from Oxfordshire to **five counties** (Oxon, Bucks, Berks, Herts, Beds — 65+ towns) ([our locations](https://www.trustontap.com/our-locations/)); 200+ carers in Oxfordshire alone; review volume growing (1,188 claimed). Co-founder Nick Grant left the board Feb 2022; small director churn otherwise. Founder publicly flagged fundraising as a persistent drag ("Raising investment... has consumed so much of my time") ([EnSpire](https://enspire.ox.ac.uk/article/william-cotton-founder-and-managing-director-of-trustontap-ltd)).
- **Interpretation (inference):** a sustainable, decade-old lifestyle/SME-scale marketplace — proof the hyperlocal model *survives* on thin capital, but also a caution that without capital it stayed regional for 10 years.

## 8. Strengths, weaknesses, lessons for CareMatch

**Strengths**
- Density-first regional strategy: 200+ carers in one county makes 24–48h matching credible; national rivals can't match that consistency locally.
- Radically transparent pricing (exact £/hr, night, live-in, admin fee) — strong conversion and trust asset in a category where agencies hide prices.
- Double-sided monetisation (18% carer + 5% family) keeps the family-facing fee psychologically small while achieving a ~23% blended take.
- Clean, honest regulatory posture — the introduction-agency limits are spelled out, which pre-empts complaints.
- Council-directory presence and personal-budget clients diversify demand beyond private pay.

**Weaknesses**
- Concierge funnel doesn't scale cheaply: phone-led matching + 3-person HQ caps growth; ten years to reach five counties.
- No self-serve product moat — no public profiles, instant booking, messaging, or app; a better-funded self-serve rival (e.g., Curam) can out-product them.
- Supply-quality variance shows up in reviews (inaccurate profiles, short-notice cancellations) — vetting claims (5% acceptance) don't fully solve marketplace QoS.
- Introduction-agency limits (no proactive carer replacement, no care direction) push continuity risk onto families.
- Chronically under-capitalised; angel top-ups only.

**Lessons for CareMatch**
1. **Launch hyperlocal and prove density before spreading.** TrustonTap's whole trust story (same carer, 24–48h match, local reviews, council listing) depends on county-level carer density. Pick one county/region, hit ~200 active carers, then replicate.
2. **Publish exact prices.** Their £20–£30/hr + fee schedule is a differentiator vs agencies and likely a big organic-conversion driver; CareMatch should be at least as transparent.
3. **Split the take rate across both sides** (small visible family fee + larger carer commission) — families anchor on the carer's rate, carers still net far more than agency pay, and the platform earns ~20%+.
4. **Get listed on council "Live Well"-style directories early** — free, credible, and reaches social workers making referrals; personal-budget clients are an accessible semi-public revenue stream that doesn't require CQC registration.
5. **Where CareMatch can beat them:** self-serve product (public carer profiles, instant booking, in-platform messaging/payments) layered on the same hyperlocal density playbook — TrustonTap validates the model but left the product surface wide open. Also: mandate carer insurance and surface verified skills to fix their review-visible quality gaps.
